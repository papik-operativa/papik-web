BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS OF BELLATERRA

``` What you should know before building or replacing in Bellaterra

Bellaterra is a sub-municipality of Cerdanyola; the major permit falls under the jurisdiction of Cerdanyola City Council with a mandatory report from the EMD. ```

Administrative framework · dual level

`` Bellaterra has the status of a Decentralised Municipal Entity (EMD) within the municipal area of Cerdanyola del Vallès. The major building permit is processed before Cerdanyola del Vallès City Council, but the EMD issues a mandatory report on specific local ordinances (tree protection, materials, façade colour, fencing). This creates a dual level of administrative authority with additional requirements. ``

Applicable POUM

`` The local urban plan (POUM) of Cerdanyola del Vallès was approved in 2014 and regulates the entire municipal area, including Bellaterra. The consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) of the Generalitat. Bellaterra has additional regulations of its own on the garden-city typology and landscape protection. ``

Common planning parameters (residential Bellaterra)

`` The minimum plot size in residential Bellaterra is typically more than 800 m² (garden-city typology). Buildable capacity is restrictive, usually between 0.3 and 0.5 m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy is low, between 20% and 30%. Maximum height is usually limited to ground floor plus one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are 5 m from the road and 5 m or more from the boundary. ``

Specific Bellaterra restrictions

`` The specific restrictions of the EMD shape a particular construction framework. The tree protection ordinance limits felling and imposes planting obligations. Materials and façade colours must be in the natural or earth tone palette. Plot fencing must follow specific typologies, with massive masonry on the road-facing façade restricted. Coordination with the Universitat Autònoma is required in sectors bordering the university campus, where the UAB Special Plan applies. ``

Demolition and new-build process

``` The demolition permit and the major building permit may be processed jointly or sequentially before Cerdanyola City Council. The documentation needed for demolition includes a technical demolition project, a safety plan, a waste management study and a specific deposit. Indicative timeframes are between 4 and 12 weeks for each permit.

BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 7 QUESTIONS

``` Q · Why does Bellaterra require special handling?

A · Bellaterra is a Decentralised Municipal Entity within Cerdanyola del Vallès. The major permit is processed before Cerdanyola City Council with a mandatory report from the EMD: a dual level of administrative authority with additional requirements on trees, materials and typology.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between demolition with new build and retrofit?

A · Retrofitting preserves the existing structure and improves performance. Demolition with new build replaces the home with a new one on the same plot. The right decision depends on structural condition, relative cost, final energy efficiency and planning compatibility. We have a complete article on the topic at /en/article-replace-vs-retrofit.

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does Bellaterra require?

A · The garden-city typology of Bellaterra has restrictive parameters: typically a minimum plot size of more than 800 m² and low occupancy (between 20% and 30%). The specific planning data sheet defines the exact parameter.

──────────────

Q · Are there restrictions on materials and façade colours?

A · Yes. The EMD Bellaterra has ordinances that restrict materials and colours to a natural or earth tone palette, restrict massive fencing on the road-facing façade, and protect the consolidated trees on the plot.

──────────────

Q · Do I need to apply for two permits (demolition and works) or just one?

A · They can be processed jointly or sequentially. The major building permit and the demolition permit have differentiated documentation but can be requested in parallel. We coordinate this with the City Council on your behalf.

──────────────

Q · How long does the process take from first contact to start of works?

A · Typically between 6 and 14 months: 3 to 6 months for the project, 2 to 3 months for the demolition permit, 2 to 3 months for the major building permit (which can overlap in time).

A · Yes. We have K-Iturbi as a certified Passivhaus case in the municipality and we have done several demolition and new-build projects in the Vallès Occidental area. Our activity in Bellaterra is recurrent. ```
