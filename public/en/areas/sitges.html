BLOCK 1 · WHY SITGES

``` Why build in Sitges

Sitges is, above any other consideration, a question of landscape. The strip of land between the Garraf Natural Park and the sea defines a residential geography that is limited, valued and protected. The consolidated areas (Terramar, Vallpineda, Aiguadolç, Vinyet, Levantina) form a mature premium residential market, with plots that in many cases have been passed down across generations.

Building in Sitges is not building in any ordinary area. It is working within a planning context where every formal decision has landscape implications, where the heritage of the historic centre demands respect, and where the coastline calls for special attention to orientation, privacy and the relationship with Mediterranean vegetation.

PAPIK does not yet have a direct case in the municipality, but works regularly on the coastal strip with nearby projects such as K-Botigues (Castelldefels). Our Passivhaus methodology and our culture of landscape integration align naturally with the level of demand the municipality expects. ```

BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS OF SITGES

``` What you should know before building in Sitges

Landscape restrictions due to the Garraf Natural Park, height limits in the historic centre and heritage protection. Sitges demands sensitivity to context. ```

Applicable POUM

`` The local urban plan (POUM) of Sitges (approximately 2006, with several subsequent amendments) regulates the entire municipal area. The consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC). A relevant part of the territory is conditioned by the Special Plan of the Garraf Natural Park, managed by the Diputació de Barcelona, and by the heritage protection regime of the historic centre. ``

Predominant land typologies

`` In the historic centre, the multi-family party-wall typology predominates with listed buildings and limited heights. The urbanisations of Terramar, Vallpineda, Vinyet, Aiguadolç and Levantina concentrate detached single-family housing on medium to large plots. Non-developable land is extensive, driven by the presence of the Garraf Natural Park, and rural land of landscape interest occupies significant areas of the municipal territory. ``

Common planning parameters

`` The minimum plot size in single-family residential is typically between 600 and 1,500 m² depending on the area, with notably larger plots in sectors such as Terramar or Vallpineda. Buildable capacity ranges between 0.3 and 0.5 m²st/m²s depending on the sub-area. Maximum occupancy is between 20% and 30% in detached residential. Height is usually ground floor plus one with roof, with specific limits in the historic centre where the historic roof line is binding. Standard setbacks are 5 m or more from the road and 3 to 5 m from the boundary depending on the planning data sheet of each area. ``

Specific restrictions · Garraf Natural Park and heritage

`` Sitges applies a dual protection regime. On the Garraf Natural Park side, projects close to the boundary may require a mandatory report from the Park Board, managed by the Diputació de Barcelona, with particular attention to the landscape integration study, earthworks and native Mediterranean vegetation. On the heritage side, the historic centre contains listed buildings and an intervention regime conditioned by a special protection plan: works on façades, roofs and volumes visible from public space are subject to technical commission criteria. The 25 m perimeter wildfire protection strip applies to plots with a forest interface. Fencing and garden vegetation are conditioned by landscape ordinances in many urbanisations. ``

Permit process

`` The electronic platform is the Sitges Town Hall e-government portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC). Indicative resolution times range between 6 and 16 weeks, with a wider margin if the project affects the protected area of the Natural Park or has an impact on listed heritage. Additional documentation may include a landscape integration study, a heritage report and a view-impact study for plots with privileged sea views. ``

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet and consult the municipal technician and, if the plot is in a heritage protection area or borders the Garraf Park, also the heritage commission or the Park Board.

BLOCK 4 · INTEGRATION PHILOSOPHY

``` Building with respect for the landscape and for heritage

Restraint, not display.

```

`` Our construction methodology in Sitges starts from a clear premise. The value of a home on this coast is not measured by the volume built or by the formal gesture, but by the quality of its relationship with the place. This conviction shapes five lines of work. Solar orientation and passive use work with winter solar gain, summer solar protection and cross ventilation aligned with the breeze regime characteristic of the Garraf coast. The palette of materials and colours is kept in a range coherent with the Mediterranean landscape (local stone, lime, timber, broken whites, light earth tones), avoiding any visual dissonance with the surroundings. Native vegetation (Aleppo pine, kermes oak, mastic, Mediterranean shrubs) acts as structural landscaping, aligned with the landscape ordinances of many urbanisations. The construction systems chosen ensure airtightness and Passivhaus thermal behaviour in a coastal climate with high ambient humidity and marine salinity, factors that demand specific construction details. The landscape impact study and, where applicable, prior dialogue with the heritage commission or the Park Board, are carried out before closing the technical project to avoid late reformulations. ``

BLOCK 5 · NOTABLE AREAS OF SITGES

``` Residential areas of the municipality

TERRAMAR is historically the most valued residential enclave in the municipality, with wide plots and a consolidated culture of Mediterranean garden. VALLPINEDA combines a wide plot with proximity to the urban core and a topography that favours view-driven orientations. AIGUADOLÇ articulates the marina area with high-end coastal residential. VINYET and LEVANTINA are premium residential enclaves with a quieter character and predominantly single-family plots. THE HISTORIC CENTRE concentrates the heritage assets, with interventions subject to a protection regime and a specific residential market of multi-family party-wall typology. Each area has its own planning regime and a different landscape sensitivity. ```

BLOCK 6 · LOCAL FAQ · 7 QUESTIONS

``` Q · What minimum plot size does Sitges require?

A · In single-family residential, typically between 600 and 1,500 m² depending on the area. Sectors such as Terramar or Vallpineda have significantly larger plots. The planning data sheet of each area defines the exact parameter.

──────────────

Q · What should I know if the plot is in the historic centre?

A · The historic centre of Sitges has a heritage protection regime with listed buildings. Interventions on façades, volumes and roofs visible from public space are subject to heritage commission criteria. The historic roof line and limited heights are binding. A longer permit process should be expected.

──────────────

Q · What is the Garraf Natural Park Special Plan and how does it affect me?

A · The Special Plan regulates landscape and environmental protection across the Garraf Natural Park, managed by the Diputació de Barcelona. If your plot borders or is close to the park boundary, the project may require a mandatory report from the Park Board, particularly with regard to earthworks, vegetation and the landscape integration study.

──────────────

Q · Do you work in Sitges often?

A · Sitges is a municipality where we do not yet have a direct PAPIK case, but we work regularly on the Garraf coastal strip (K-Botigues in Castelldefels) and we have references of rigour in landscape restrictions (K-Matadepera). Our methodology aligns with the level of demand the municipality expects.

──────────────

Q · Is a wildfire protection strip required?

A · On plots with a forest interface, yes: 25 m perimeter with vegetation management according to wildfire regulations. Particularly relevant on urbanisations bordering the Garraf Park. It is part of the ordinary maintenance of the plot and is the owner's responsibility.

──────────────

Q · Can flat roofs be built in Sitges?

A · It depends on the area. In the historic centre, pitched roofs with Arab tile are mandatory. In urbanisations such as Terramar or Vallpineda, flat roofs are common and compatible with a contemporary Mediterranean language. The planning data sheet of each area specifies the regime.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building in Sitges and on another coast?

A · Sitges combines protected landscape (Garraf Park), historic heritage (historic centre) and a premium residential market that has been consolidated for decades. The process is more demanding than on many other Catalan coasts. The value of the land and the singularity of the context justify the level of demand of the project. ```
