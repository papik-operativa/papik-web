BLOCK 1 · WHY VILASSAR DE MAR

``` Why building in Vilassar de Mar now

Vilassar de Mar is a town of about twenty-one thousand inhabitants with a consolidated mid-premium residential profile. Its strength comes from three combined factors: an accessible seafront, a RENFE R1 commuter station in the centre with continuous frequency to Barcelona, and a commercial and service fabric scaled for a town with its own identity.

The residential market is concentrated in detached and semi-detached single-family homes in the upper part of the municipality, with plots that allow a complete family brief. Areas such as the Vimar sector or the Camí del Mig axis combine favourable orientation, gentle topography and good inland connection with the N-II and C-32 networks. For families seeking a balance between coastal life and metropolitan mobility, Vilassar is one of the most stable options in the Maresme.

BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

`` What you should know before building in Vilassar de Mar ``

Current local urban plan (POUM)

`` The local urban plan (POUM) of Vilassar de Mar was approved in 2003 and has been subject to subsequent specific amendments. The consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) of the Generalitat and through the municipal planning portal. ``

Predominant land typologies

`` The detached and semi-detached single-family typology predominates in the upper part of the municipality and in sectors such as Vimar, with medium-sized plots. The party-wall multi-family typology is concentrated in the historic centre and along the axis parallel to the coastline, with consolidated frontages. Terraced residential sectors appear in more recent derived plans. Non-developable land remains protected on the northern slopes, in transition areas towards Cabrils and Vilassar de Dalt. ``

Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

`` The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential is typically between 300 and 600 m² depending on the sub-zone. Net buildable capacity ranges between 0.4 and 0.6 m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy is between 30% and 40%. Standard height is ground floor plus one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are 3 to 5 m from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary. ``

Landscape restrictions

`` Proximity to the seafront generates specific constraints on orientation, heights and finishes for facades visible from the promenade. Sectors bordering the RENFE railway line have railway easements. Consolidated pine vegetation in the north of the municipality is protected by municipal ordinance in certain areas. ``

Permit process

`` The electronic platform is the Vilassar de Mar e-government portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC). Indicative resolution times range between 6 and 12 weeks, with variability depending on the workload of the planning office and any sectoral consultations from the coastal authority or heritage. The ICIO tax applies according to the current Tax Ordinance. ``

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

BLOCK 4 · NOTABLE AREAS OF VILASSAR DE MAR

``` Residential areas of the municipality

THE HISTORIC CENTRE concentrates the party-wall multi-family fabric and consolidated single-family homes around the urban core, with options for deep retrofit and "demolish and rebuild" interventions. THE VIMAR SECTOR is a mixed single-family residential area with medium-sized plots, well oriented and with gentle topography. CAMÍ DEL MIG forms a consolidated single-family residential axis crossing the municipality from coast to mountain, with detached and semi-detached typologies. THE UPPER PART is characterised by plots with moderate slope, sea views and a quieter residential profile, well suited to complete family briefs. ```
