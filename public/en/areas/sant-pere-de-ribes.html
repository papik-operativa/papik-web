BLOCK 1 · WHY SANT PERE DE RIBES

``` Why building in Sant Pere de Ribes now

Sant Pere de Ribes is a municipality of around thirty thousand inhabitants with three distinct nuclei: Ribes (the administrative centre), Les Roquetes (highest density, expansion axis) and a network of dispersed estates around them. Its proximity to Sitges, with direct exits to the C-15 and C-32 motorways, places the municipality in a strategic position: residential life with its own identity and quick access to Sitges' cultural and service offer.

The residential market offers a favourable share of single-family plots in areas such as Vallpineda or Can Pere de la Plana, with land prices typically lower than those in Sitges or on the seafront. For families seeking a Passivhaus home in the Garraf without paying the seafront premium, Sant Pere de Ribes is one of the most balanced options.

BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

`` What you should know before building in Sant Pere de Ribes ``

Current local urban plan (POUM)

`` The local urban plan (POUM) of Sant Pere de Ribes was approved in 2007 and has been subject to subsequent specific amendments, including sectoral revisions of housing estates. The consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) of the Generalitat and through the municipal planning portal. ``

Predominant land typologies

`` The detached single-family typology predominates in the historic dispersed estates and in sectors such as Vallpineda or Can Pere de la Plana. The party-wall multi-family typology is concentrated in the Ribes and Les Roquetes nuclei, with consolidated urban fabric. Terraced residential sectors appear in more recent derived plans. Non-developable land remains protected in the Garraf massif, to the south of the municipality, with links to the Natural Park. ``

Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

`` The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential is typically between 400 and 1,000 m² depending on the sub-zone, with more generous sizes in consolidated estates. Net buildable capacity ranges between 0.3 and 0.5 m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy is between 25% and 35%. Standard height is ground floor plus one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are 4 to 5 m from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary. ``

Landscape restrictions

`` Proximity to the Garraf Natural Park generates specific constraints in the bordering sectors: volume, materials, exterior finishes and colour treatment. Some historic estates have their own ordinances regulating typology, perimeter walls and vegetation. The rugged hill topography requires a geotechnical study with stability analysis on many plots. ``

Permit process

`` The electronic platform is the Sant Pere de Ribes e-government portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC). Indicative resolution times range between 8 and 16 weeks, with variability depending on the workload of the planning office and any sectoral consultations from the environment or heritage authorities. The ICIO tax applies according to the current Tax Ordinance. ``

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

BLOCK 4 · NOTABLE AREAS OF SANT PERE DE RIBES

``` Residential areas of the municipality

RIBES CORE is the administrative and commercial centre, with a consolidated party-wall multi-family fabric and urban single-family homes with options for retrofit or "demolish and rebuild" interventions. LES ROQUETES concentrates the highest residential density and is the most recent expansion axis, with mixed typologies and medium-sized single-family plots. VALLPINEDA is a historic estate with wide plots, hill topography and a quiet residential character, well suited to complete family briefs. CAN PERE DE LA PLANA is a consolidated single-family residential sector with good orientation and favourable topography. The DISPERSED ESTATES around the urban core offer generous plots with a favourable quality-to-price ratio, always subject to their own ordinances which should be verified case by case. ```

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Sant Pere de Ribes?

A · It depends on the area and the estate. In detached single-family it is typically between 400 and 1,000 m². Some historic estates have their own parameters. The specific planning data sheet of the plot defines the exact applicable value.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Sant Pere de Ribes?

A · Resolution usually takes between 8 and 16 weeks from the complete submission of the documentation. Sectors bordering the Garraf Natural Park may require environmental consultations that extend the deadline.

──────────────

Q · Can Passivhaus be built on sloped plots?

A · Yes. We have experience with rugged plots in the Vallès and the Garraf. The key is the initial geotechnical study and a structural project that takes advantage of the slope: usable basements, stepped terraces, a brief that follows the contour. The Passivhaus construction system adapts to these constraints without losing energy performance.
