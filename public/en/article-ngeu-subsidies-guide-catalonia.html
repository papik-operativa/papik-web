NGEU subsidies guide for retrofit in Catalonia

Practical guide to Next Generation EU programs 3, 4 and 5 for Catalan property owners. Programs explained, eligibility, application process, common mistakes and calendar of deadlines. Application to single-family housing and condominiums.

General framework · what residential NGEU funds are

The Recovery, Transformation and Resilience Plan approved in 2021 allocates around three thousand five hundred million euros to housing retrofit, structured into six operational programs (1 to 6). In the residential sphere, the three most relevant for the end owner are Program 3 (building-level retrofit), Program 4 (energy efficiency improvement in dwellings) and Program 5 (Building Logbook and retrofit projects). In Catalonia, the state-level call is implemented through specific orders of the Agència de l'Habitatge and, in some cases, with co-funding from the Department of Social Rights and Housing.

The three programs are compatible with personal income tax (IRPF) deductions of up to sixty per cent (Royal Decree-Law 19/2021), subject to an annual individual cap. The combined subsidy plus deduction can cover between forty and seventy-five per cent of the total cost of the works, depending on the case and on the level of energy improvement achieved.

Program 3 · building-level retrofit

Program 3 has the largest budget allocation. It covers integral energy retrofit works in collective residential buildings, that is, condominiums. The central condition is to achieve a verified reduction of energy demand of thirty per cent in Mediterranean climates (zones B and C, applicable to most of Catalonia). The maximum subsidy amount ranges between forty and eighty per cent of the eligible cost depending on the degree of improvement achieved.

Eligible works typically include thermal insulation of facade and roof, window replacement, photovoltaic installation, air-source heat pumps (aerothermal), mechanical ventilation with heat recovery and, in some cases, common installations (lifts, accessibility). The applicant is the community of owners, through its property administrator or legal representative. The application requires a stamped technical project with calculation of current and projected demand after the works (initial and final EPC), execution budgets and minutes of approval from the owners' meeting.

In Catalonia the ordinary submission deadline is open until June 2026 with a planned extension, subject to budget availability. Updated details are published by the Agència de l'Habitatge de Catalunya. PAPIK has handled Program 3 applications in condominiums of Vallès Occidental and Maresme; the methodological detail is developed under the retrofit service and the communities page.

Program 4 · individual dwelling

Program 4 is the equivalent for owners of single-family homes or individual flats (the owner applies for their flat within a building, with no need for community approval). The central condition is, likewise, to achieve a verified reduction of thirty per cent in energy demand, with a maximum subsidy ceiling of fourteen thousand euros per dwelling and forty per cent of the cost.

Unlike Program 3, Program 4 covers works inside the dwelling: window replacement, interior insulation, individual ventilation, thermal installations. It does not cover works on common elements. For flat owners, this means that Program 4 improves the interior skin of their apartment; Program 3, in contrast, improves the shared exterior skin of the building. The two programs are compatible with each other if the community opts for joint execution.

The applicant is the owner or the usufructuary, with national ID/NIE and proof of ownership (deeds, cadastral certificate). The application requires a current EPC issued by a competent technician, a project document (in some cases only a costed memorandum), a detailed budget and a sworn declaration of not having received other incompatible aids for the same item.

Program 5 · building logbook and projects

Program 5 covers two preparatory products: the drafting of the Existing Building Logbook (a document that gathers construction features, installations and state of conservation) and the drafting of the technical retrofit project. The amount is modest (between seven hundred and four thousand euros depending on the case) but essential: it serves to fund the preliminary phase of any Program 3 or Program 4 intervention.

For a community considering an integral retrofit but which does not yet have a technical project, applying for Program 5 as a first step is a rational decision. It funds the study phase without commitment to execute works, and produces technical documentation that will serve both to decide on Program 3 and for any future sale or appraisal operations.

Cross-cutting requirements and incompatibilities

All three programs share cross-cutting requirements. The building or dwelling must be older than the year 2000 (in some programs, 2007). The use must be habitual residential, not second home. The applicant must be up to date with tax and Social Security obligations. The works must start within the planned deadlines (typically twelve months from the award) and be certified with a final energy audit verifying the projected improvement.

The main incompatibilities affect concurrent subsidies: you cannot simultaneously receive Program 3 and a MOVES grant for the same item (for example, the same heat pump), but it is compatible with tax deductions and, in general, with other programs that do not cover the same line. The usual rule is: a single invoice cannot be charged to two programs at once; different invoices for different concepts are compatible.

Application process step by step

The ordinary process follows six phases. First, prior assessment: the owner or community commissions a current energy performance certificate and, where appropriate, a feasibility study of the projected improvement. Second, technical project: drafted by a competent architect or engineer, with professional stamp and calculation of current and projected demand. Third, community approval (Program 3 only): owners' meeting, vote with the majorities established by the Horizontal Property Law and minutes recording the corresponding approval. Fourth, electronic application: submission via the Agència de l'Habitatge portal with all attached documentation. Fifth, resolution and award: the indicative timeframe is three to six months from submission. Sixth, execution and justification: the works are carried out, the improvement is certified with the post-works EPC and the final justification is submitted to collect the subsidy.

In practice, counting from the initial decision to effective payment of the subsidy can take between twelve and twenty-four months. This timeline must be planned into the project: the property must be able to fund the works initially (with own resources or bridge financing), because the subsidy is paid afterwards.

Common mistakes that invalidate the application

Six recurring mistakes invalidate or delay the application. First, submitting a budget without professional stamp or without item-by-item detail; second, failing to properly evidence the projected thirty per cent EPC improvement; third, starting the works before the award resolution, which voids the right to receive the aid; fourth, submitting without formal community approval in the case of Program 3 (the minutes must be registered and dated); fifth, supplier invoices from companies not up to date with tax and Social Security obligations, which is checked automatically; sixth, failing to file the final justification within the established deadline (typically three months from the end of the works).

Each of these mistakes can result in the total loss of the subsidy, with the additional problem that some expenses may already have been incurred. Prior planning with a team familiar with the protocols significantly reduces this risk.

Current calendar (reference)

As of April 2026, the operational calendar is as follows. Program 3 and Program 4 are open with a submission window running until June 2026, with an extension foreseen subject to budget availability. Program 5 maintains continuous submission while credit remains. Extensions are published one month in advance on the Agència de l'Habitatge portal.

For a community or owner planning to start the process now, the realistic horizon for effective payment is 2027 or 2028, depending on the complexity of the works. This article will be reviewed periodically as regulatory or budgetary updates are published.

Frequently asked questions

Who can submit the application?

The owner, the usufructuary or, in the case of Program 3, the community of owners through its administrator. Tenants or occupants without legal title cannot apply.

Can it be combined with a green mortgage?

Yes. The green mortgage is a financial product used to fund the part not covered by the subsidy. Both instruments are complementary and are coordinated within the project's timeline.

What is the difference between EnerPHit and a standard Program 3?

EnerPHit is the Passivhaus certification for retrofit; a standard Program 3 requires a thirty per cent improvement, not necessarily Passivhaus. An EnerPHit retrofit comfortably exceeds the Program 3 threshold and can access higher subsidy tiers (up to eighty per cent in some cases).

What happens if the thirty per cent improvement is not achieved?

The aid is not paid or is reduced proportionally. That is why post-works EPC verification is critical and must be planned at the design phase with a technical margin.
