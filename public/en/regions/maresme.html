BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE MARESME

``` Why build in the Maresme now

The Maresme combines proximity to Barcelona with a coastal residential context and a very characteristic topography between the Coastal Range (Serralada Litoral) and the sea. The municipalities of the lower Maresme (Alella, Tiana, Premià, Vilassar) act as a residential ring around Barcelona; those in the centre (Mataró, Argentona, Cabrils) combine the role of regional capital with selective residential markets; and those of the northern Maresme (Sant Andreu de Llavaneres) offer larger plots with slope and mature vegetation.

The Mediterranean coastal climate imposes specific requirements. The main demand for passive housing on the Maresme is not heating but passive cooling and summer solar control. We have proven experience in complex orientations, exterior solar shading, and cross night-time ventilation, all critical elements in this climate context.

The slope-rich plots common across the Maresme require foundation and topographic adaptation solutions that have been part of our project catalogue for years. ```

BLOCK 3 · COMMON PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

``` Common planning framework in the Maresme

The Maresme presents clear common denominators: sloped topography, non-developable land with strong landscape value and coastal restrictions on first-line municipalities. ```

``` Most municipalities in the Maresme have POUMs (Catalan Municipal Planning Master Plans) approved between 2002 and 2018, with specific amendments mainly driven by residential pressure and landscape considerations. The consolidated version is publicly available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) and on the corresponding municipal urban-planning portals.

Typical residential single-family buildable ratios on the Maresme sit between 0.3 and 0.5 m² of floor area per m² of plot, slightly lower than in Vallès Occidental, with coverage between 25% and 35% and cornice heights of ground plus one floor (approximately 7 m) plus roof. Minimum plot sizes range from 600 to 1,500 m² depending on the municipality and sector. Setbacks are typically 3 to 5 m.

All coastal municipalities (Premià, Vilassar de Mar, Mataró, Llavaneres) are subject to coastal legislation and to the maritime-terrestrial public domain protection easement, with specific conditions for plots near the shoreline. Several municipalities (Cabrils, Alella, Tiana, Llavaneres, Argentona) have landscape restrictions associated with the Coastal Range and the corresponding PEIN (plan of natural interest).

Major works permit applications follow the standard procedure across Catalonia: technical project, safety study, waste management plan and municipal fees. Indicative resolution times sit between 6 and 14 weeks, slightly higher than the Vallès average, particularly when there is coastal or landscape impact. ```

⚠ Any specific data requires verification on the plot's urban-planning record and on the current municipal POUM.

BLOCK 5 · REGIONAL FAQ

``` Frequently asked questions · Maresme

Q · Do you work across the entire Maresme?

A · Yes. We have documented projects in Sant Andreu de Llavaneres, Argentona, Premià, Mataró, Vilassar and other municipalities. Our headquarters are in Sant Cugat, but we dispatch technical and production teams to the region with agility.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building on the Maresme and in Vallès Occidental?

A · The main difference is climatic. On the Maresme the critical demand is passive cooling and summer solar control, whereas in Vallès Occidental the main challenge is winter heating. The topography also changes, with much more presence of slope and complex plots on the Maresme.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions due to proximity to the sea?

A · Yes. Coastal legislation establishes a maritime-terrestrial public domain protection easement that affects plots near the shoreline. These constraints condition heights, volumes and materials. Our experience with coastal projects includes managing these restrictions.

──────────────

Q · What is the minimum plot size required on the Maresme?

A · It depends on the municipality and sector. In detached single-family residential it usually ranges between 600 and 1,500 m². The plot's urban-planning record defines the exact parameter.

──────────────

Q · How long does a major works permit take on the Maresme?

A · Resolution typically takes between 6 and 14 weeks from a complete documentary submission, slightly longer than the Vallès Occidental average. Times can extend on plots with coastal or landscape impact. ```
