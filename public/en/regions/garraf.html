BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE GARRAF

``` Why build in the Garraf now

The Garraf is the coastal residential region south of the Barcelona metropolitan area, with a dual fabric between urban cores combining tourism and residential character (Sitges, Vilanova) and dispersed development sectors with large plots (Sant Pere de Ribes and mountain areas). The Garraf Natural Park conditions a substantial part of the territory and establishes a landscape component very present across the entire region.

Strong connections to Barcelona (C-32 motorway, R2 South commuter rail) keep the Garraf as a priority destination for families seeking to build away from metropolitan pressure but with quick access to the capital. The plot market is selective in consolidated coastal areas (Sitges, Vilanova first line) and has more headroom in development sectors and in Sant Pere de Ribes and Cubelles.

The Mediterranean coastal climate imposes specific demands: demanding summer solar control, salt-spray protection on plots near the sea, and cross natural ventilation. Our Passivhaus and Eskimohaus® methodology (PAPIK's proprietary low-energy system) responds directly to these requirements. ```

BLOCK 2 · MUNICIPALITIES IN THE REGION WHERE WE BUILD

``` Municipalities in the Garraf where we have built

We build in the four main municipalities of the region. We do not yet have a dedicated landing for any Garraf municipality. ```

Vilanova i la Geltrú

`` The regional capital, Vilanova combines a historic urban core, a coastal sector with multi-family character, and residential sectors on the periphery. K-Botigues is one of our documented projects in the territory. Multi-family energy retrofits are frequent in blocks from the 1970s and 1980s within the core and along the seafront. ``

Sitges

`` Sitges is the selective residential municipality of the region, with a mature market where controlled substitution (demolition with new build) is frequent on city-centre plots and in mountain developments (Quint Mar, Garraf 2). The heritage component of the historic core conditions interventions on protected dwellings. ``

Sant Pere de Ribes

`` Sant Pere de Ribes combines two cores (Ribes and Les Roquetes) with dispersed development sectors across the municipality. Large single-family plots are common, and we see occasional opportunities for residential new-build and for retrofitting houses from the 1970s. ``

Cubelles

`` Cubelles is the transitional municipality between the Garraf and the coastal Penedès, with a single-family residential component and a more affordable plot market than Sitges or Vilanova. The proximity to the Foix river introduces hydraulic constraints on some plots. ``

BLOCK 3 · COMMON PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

``` Common planning framework in the Garraf

The Garraf shares very clear planning characteristics: a coastal component, the presence of the natural park and frequent sloped topography. ```

``` The four Garraf municipalities have POUMs (Catalan Municipal Planning Master Plans) approved between 2001 and 2017, with specific amendments derived from the landscape component and residential pressure. The consolidated version is publicly available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) and on each municipal urban-planning portal.

Typical residential single-family buildable ratios in the Garraf sit between 0.3 and 0.5 m² of floor area per m² of plot, with coverage between 25% and 35% and cornice heights of ground plus one floor (approximately 7 m) plus roof. Minimum plot sizes range from 500 to 1,500 m² depending on the municipality and sector. In Sitges, selective plots can have stricter parameters depending on the specific urban-planning record.

All coastal municipalities (Vilanova, Sitges, Cubelles) are subject to coastal legislation and to the maritime-terrestrial public domain protection easement. The Garraf Natural Park affects non-developable land within Sitges, Sant Pere de Ribes and bordering sectors, with specific conditions on roofs, fencing, façade colour and native vegetation.

Major works permit applications follow the standard procedure across Catalonia. Indicative resolution times sit between 6 and 14 weeks, with possible extensions on plots affected by coastal, landscape or natural park constraints. ```

⚠ Any specific data requires verification on the plot's urban-planning record and on the current municipal POUM.

BLOCK 5 · REGIONAL FAQ

``` Frequently asked questions · Garraf

Q · Do you work in the four Garraf municipalities?

A · Yes. We have documented projects in the territory and the technical and logistical capacity to operate in Vilanova i la Geltrú, Sitges, Sant Pere de Ribes and Cubelles. Our headquarters are in Sant Cugat, which lets us dispatch teams via the AP-7 and C-32 with agility.

──────────────

Q · Are there significant planning differences between Garraf municipalities?

A · Yes. Sitges has the most selective residential market and the strictest urban-planning records. Vilanova has a more multi-family fabric in the urban area and single-family on the periphery. Sant Pere de Ribes and Cubelles offer more headroom for new-build on single-family plots.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions from the Garraf Natural Park?

A · Yes. The Natural Park affects non-developable land within Sitges, Sant Pere de Ribes and bordering sectors, with specific conditions on roofs, fencing, façade colour and native vegetation. We verify these constraints on a case-by-case basis.

──────────────

Q · Which energy standard do you recommend for the Garraf climate?

A · The Mediterranean coastal climate of the Garraf has a critical demand for summer cooling and solar protection rather than for heating. The Passivhaus or Eskimohaus® standard, with our specifications for exterior solar shading and cross natural ventilation, is directly applicable.

──────────────

Q · How long does a major works permit take in the Garraf?

A · Resolution typically takes between 6 and 14 weeks from a complete documentary submission, with possible extensions on plots affected by coastal, landscape or natural park constraints. ```
