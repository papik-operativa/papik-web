BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE ANOIA

``` Why build in the Anoia now

The Anoia is an inland county of central Catalonia, articulated around Igualada as its capital and with a diversified economic fabric combining industrial heritage (paper, textile, tanned leather) with a growing agricultural and logistics component. The A-2 motorway and the planned Llobregat-Anoia railway extension (FGC) keep the county as a viable residential alternative for families seeking affordable land within reasonable reach of Barcelona.

The terrain is varied: the Conca d'Òdena basin around Igualada and the southern municipalities (Hostalets de Pierola, Piera) offer large single-family plots, while El Bruc and the Montserrat area face significant landscape constraints. The northern municipalities (Calaf, Calonge de Segarra) belong to the rural central Catalan dynamic.

The moderate continental climate of the Anoia, with cold winters and hot summers, requires demanding thermal envelopes in both directions. Our Passivhaus and Eskimohaus® methodology (PAPIK's proprietary low-energy system) is directly applicable, with particular attention to summer thermal control and winter comfort. ```

BLOCK 2 · MUNICIPALITIES IN THE COUNTY WHERE WE BUILD

``` Municipalities in the Anoia where we have built

We build in the principal municipalities of the county. We do not yet have a dedicated landing page for any Anoia municipality. ```

Igualada

`` County capital, Igualada combines a historic urban core, a nineteenth-century extension and industrial sectors partially converted to residential use (el Rec, Cal Granotes). K-Igualada is our documented project in the municipality and exemplifies our capacity to intervene in town-centre plots with heritage considerations. Energy retrofit is a frequent commission in multifamily blocks from the 1960s and 1970s. ``

Hostalets de Pierola

`` Hostalets de Pierola is a municipality on the southern fringe of the county, with dispersed development sectors and large single-family plots. Proximity to the Can Mata palaeontological site and the natural component of the territory shape a selective new-build residential market. ``

Piera

`` Piera is the second urban core of the Anoia by population, with extensive developments in the south of the municipality and a dual residential market between primary and second residence. New-build single-family work is common here. ``

Vilanova del Camí

`` Vilanova del Camí borders Igualada to the south and has a consolidated multifamily fabric with single-family sectors at the periphery. Multifamily energy retrofit is the most frequent commission. ``

Capellades

`` Capellades, historically tied to the paper industry, retains a compact urban core and residential sectors at the periphery with medium-sized plots. Intervention in 1950s and 1960s housing has real headroom here. ``

BLOCK 3 · COMMON URBAN-PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

``` Common planning framework in the Anoia

Anoia municipalities share inherited industrial character, varied terrain and lower urban-planning pressure than the metropolitan counties. ```

``` Most Anoia municipalities have POUMs (municipal master plans) approved between 2002 and 2018, with frequent partial amendments in industrial sectors converted to residential use (especially in Igualada). The consolidated version is available at the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) and at each municipality's planning portal.

Typical single-family residential floor-area ratio in the Anoia runs between 0.4 and 0.6 m² of built area per m² of plot, with plot occupation between 30% and 40% and cornice heights of ground-plus-one (around 7 m) plus roof. Minimum plot size ranges from 400 to 1,500 m² depending on municipality and sector. Igualada has urban-extension sectors with specific multifamily parameters that should be consulted in the urban-planning fact sheet.

Proximity to the Sant Llorenç del Munt Natural Park (El Bruc), Montserrat Natural Park and Natura 2000 network sites conditions intervention on adjacent non-developable land. Municipalities with industrial heritage may have plots subject to the Catalan Inventory of Potentially Contaminated Soils, which requires prior verification before any demolish-and-rebuild project.

Major-works permit applications follow the standard Catalan procedure. Indicative resolution times range from 6 to 14 weeks from complete documentation submission, depending on municipality and complexity of the case. ```

⚠ Any specific figure requires verification on the property's urban-planning fact sheet and the current municipal POUM.

BLOCK 5 · COUNTY FAQ

``` Frequently asked questions · Anoia

Q · Do you work across the entire Anoia or only in Igualada?

A · We work across the entire county. Our head office is in Sant Cugat, which lets us travel to Igualada and the Anoia municipalities along the A-2 with agility. We have proven experience in the territory and the technical capacity to act in any municipality of the county.

──────────────

Q · Does the industrial heritage of the municipalities affect construction?

A · On plots with an industrial past, yes. Sectors converted to residential use may be listed in the Catalan Inventory of Potentially Contaminated Soils, which requires prior soil characterisation before any demolish-and-rebuild project. We verify this case by case as part of our initial due diligence.

──────────────

Q · What price difference is there between the Anoia and the Vallès?

A · Construction cost is similar. The main difference is land price: the Anoia has clearly more affordable land per square metre than Vallès Occidental or Maresme, making it a viable option for families seeking large plots within a more contained budget.

──────────────

Q · What energy standard do you recommend for the Anoia climate?

A · The moderate continental climate of the Anoia requires a balanced thermal envelope, with particular attention to thermal inertia and summer solar control. The Passivhaus standard and our Eskimohaus® implementation are directly applicable here, with adjustments to insulation thickness and external solar protection.

──────────────

Q · How long does the major-works permit take in the Anoia?

A · Resolution typically takes between 6 and 14 weeks from complete documentation submission, with possible extensions on plots with environmental or industrial constraints. ```
