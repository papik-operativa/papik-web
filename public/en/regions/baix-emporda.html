BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE BAIX EMPORDÀ

``` Why build in the Baix Empordà now

The Baix Empordà is the heart of central Costa Brava, with a residential fabric divided between coastal cores with strong tourism and premium second-home character (Calonge, Sant Antoni, Palamós, Palafrugell, Begur, Pals) and an Empordà inland with historic cores of high heritage quality (La Bisbal, Peratallada, Cruïlles, Monells). The Mediterranean landscape component, with the Montgrí massif to the north and the Gavarres range inland, shapes a strong landscape character throughout.

Connections with Barcelona (AP-7 motorway, the C-32 currently to Tossa with planned extension) and with Girona (AP-7, N-II) keep the county as a priority destination for families seeking quality second residence or premium primary residence. The plot market is selective in the first line (Sa Riera, Aiguablava, Cala Sant Francesc) and has more headroom in consolidated developments (Mas Pinell, inland Calonge, Romanyà).

The Mediterranean coastal climate of the Baix Empordà imposes specific demands: demanding summer solar control, salt-spray protection on plots near the sea, tramontana wind management on north façades and cross natural ventilation. Our Passivhaus and Eskimohaus® methodology (PAPIK's proprietary low-energy system) responds directly to these requirements. ```

BLOCK 3 · COMMON URBAN-PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

``` Common planning framework in the Baix Empordà

The Baix Empordà shares a coastal component, presence of natural parks and a highly valuable heritage fabric that conditions intervention across most of the territory. ```

``` Baix Empordà municipalities have POUMs (municipal master plans) approved between 2002 and 2018, with frequent partial amendments in coastal sectors and protected historic cores. The consolidated version is available at the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) and at each municipality's planning portal.

Single-family residential floor-area ratio in the Baix Empordà typically runs between 0.2 and 0.4 m² of built area per m² of plot on selective coastal plots, and between 0.4 and 0.6 m²/m² in consolidated inland development sectors. Maximum occupation ranges from 20% to 35% by sector, with cornice heights of ground-plus-one (around 7 m) plus roof. Minimum plots in the first line can exceed 1,500 m² and carry special parameters for setback, colour and native vegetation.

The Montgrí, Medes Islands and Baix Ter Natural Park affects the municipal areas of Torroella, l'Escala (border with Alt Empordà) and Pals. The Gavarres National Interest Natural Site (PNIN) affects inland Baix Empordà sectors (inland Calonge, Cruïlles, Romanyà). All coastal municipalities are subject to coastal legislation and to the public maritime-terrestrial domain protection easement.

Major-works permit applications follow the standard Catalan procedure. Indicative resolution times run from 8 to 16 weeks from complete documentation submission, with extensions common on plots with coastal, heritage or Natural Park constraints. ```

⚠ Any specific figure requires verification on the property's urban-planning fact sheet and the current municipal POUM.

A · The Mediterranean coastal climate of the Baix Empordà has a critical demand for summer cooling, salt-spray protection and tramontana wind management on north façades.
