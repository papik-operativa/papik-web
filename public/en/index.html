BLOCK 3 · OUR HISTORY

``` Thirty years building the future of the home

In 1994, Papik Fisas Moreno founded PAPIK in Sant Cugat del Vallès (greater Barcelona area) with a vision that was uncommon in the sector at the time: homes designed to last, not to sell. Construction with craft, natural materials, energy efficiency before that phrase had a name of its own.

In 2010 we built the second certified Passivhaus (the German low-energy building standard) home in Catalonia. In 2014 we developed our proprietary Eskimohaus® system, the result of two decades of accumulated experience. Today, more than 100 homes built and 36 documented on the website mean that every new project starts from a solid, verifiable foundation.

We continue to work in Catalonia, the Balearic Islands and Andorra with the same principle that guided the first project: a well-built home is the most profitable long-term investment, both for those who live in it and for those who will come after.

BLOCK 4 · ESKIMOHAUS® SYSTEM

``` Eskimohaus®, our proprietary construction system

After thirty years of building, we have developed a system that combines the principles of the German Passivhaus standard with Mediterranean materials and climate. This is not about applying an imported technology, but about integrating an international methodology with the specific conditions of the territory.

Four principles, nothing superfluous

```

Column 1 · THERMAL ENVELOPE

`` Continuous insulation, elimination of thermal bridges. We reduce energy losses before thinking about generating energy, because an efficient home starts at the wall. ``

Column 2 · CONTROLLED AIRTIGHTNESS

`` Verified with a post-construction blower-door test. An airtight home is not a home that does not breathe; it is a home that controls how it breathes. ``

Column 3 · VENTILATION WITH HEAT RECOVERY

`` Constant fresh air without energy loss. Zehnder heat recovery systems keep the home healthy throughout the year. ``

Column 4 · EFFICIENT STRUCTURE

`` Cross-laminated timber (CLT), light-frame timber or hybrid systems depending on the project. Natural, renewable materials with minimal impact on the plot. ``

``` An Eskimohaus® home consumes up to 90% less energy than a conventional build.

NOTES FOR DEV

Images

Hero: request from PAPIK an editorial photo of K-del Carril or K-Vall d'Or with premium edit. In the meantime, provisional photo from the existing gallery. Project cards: PAPIK will supply edited images. Provisional: existing images with uniform CSS treatment. Block 3 history: photo of the founder (verify permission) or photo of the workshop.

Animations

Hero with light fade-in on load. Block 1 cards with hover that lifts 2 to 4 pixels maximum, without saturating. Block 5 project cards with same hover behaviour and a 5% image zoom. No scrolling parallax and no excessive GSAP, in line with the cross-cutting minimalism rule.

Accessibility

Minimum WCAG AA contrast on all text. Visible keyboard focus on all CTAs. Descriptive alt text on all images (no decorative ones). <button> element for CTAs that do not navigate, <a> for CTAs that do.

JSON-LD schema to insert

See seo-internal/schemas/01-organization-homepage.json. Replace all {{ ... }} placeholders with real values before publishing.

Performance targets

LCP under 2.5 seconds (preload the hero image, WebP or AVIF format). INP under 200 milliseconds. CLS under 0.1. Critical CSS inlined. JS deferred. Map with lazy load (does not load until scroll).

PRE-PUBLICATION CHECKLIST

Mandatory checks before publishing the home page:

All internal links point to valid URLs. Hero image optimised as WebP with preload and descriptive alt. Schema validated with the Rich Results Test. Hreflang correctly configured (CA, ES, EN). NAP byte-identical in the footer. Newsletter form working with GDPR checkbox. Phone and email clickable. OG image 1200x630 generated. Mobile testing at 320px, 375px, 414px, 768px and 1024px or above. Screen reader test (VoiceOver or NVDA). LCP under 2.5 seconds measured with PageSpeed Insights.
