K-SANDAL: passive housing in Collserola Natural Park

What K-SANDAL is

K-SANDAL is a development of single-family Passivhaus (passive house standard) homes located on a plot integrated into Collserola Natural Park. Each home is designed with the Eskimohaus® system, based on a lightweight timber-frame construction with continuous insulation, controlled airtightness and ventilation with heat recovery, in order to achieve the Passivhaus standard of the Darmstadt Institute.

Unlike a custom-built construction (see our Construction service), a development is a finished product. The home is designed, planned and built by PAPIK before the end buyer signs, and the buyer assumes no construction risk, no schedule risk, and no budget risk. They move in the day they sign the deed of sale.

Technical characteristics

K-SANDAL uses PAPIK's Eskimohaus® system, which combines continuous insulation without structural thermal bridges, controlled airtightness (verified with a post-construction blower-door (airtightness) test), ventilation with heat recovery using a Zehnder system or equivalent, high-performance windows with triple glazing and inert-gas chambers, and a structure of natural and renewable materials compatible with the protected environment.

Each home is designed to achieve the full Passivhaus standard, with energy consumption up to 90% lower than that of an equivalent conventional construction. The target final energy classification is A, with potential for partial energy autonomy thanks to the integration of photovoltaic panels and self-consumption systems.

Landscape integration is a structuring element, not a decorative one. The palette of materials and colours is defined to be compatible with the Collserola Natural Park (PEIN) ordinance, native vegetation forms part of the natural enclosure of each plot, and distances to roads and boundaries have been adjusted to minimise visual impact on the protected environment. The project phase included direct coordination with the Natural Park authority.

Why Collserola requires special treatment

Collserola Natural Park is a Plan of Areas of Natural Interest (PEIN) declared in 1992. PEIN is the Catalan regional framework that protects ecologically sensitive land from unrestricted development; comparable in function to a regional nature reserve designation. Building within it, or in adjacent zones with planning impact, involves restrictions that a standard project does not contemplate.

The Collserola Special Plan regulates typologies, materials and densities; roofs are usually pitched with ceramic tile or similar; façade colour is restricted to the natural and earth-tone range by ordinance; plot boundaries are usually executed with native vegetation rather than solid masonry; distances to adjacent non-developable land are greater than usual; and projects close to the park boundary may require a binding report from the Natural Park Consortium.

These ordinances are not an obstacle but a guide that orients the project toward integration. K-SANDAL is conceived with these rules as a starting point, not as an imposed limitation.

The process that made K-SANDAL possible

K-SANDAL is the result of a process spanning more than eighteen months that combines several roles within PAPIK. Plot identification was carried out by our self-development department, which located the plot and validated buildability under the applicable POUM (municipal urban-planning master plan). The architectural design was developed by the in-house architecture team, with specific sensitivity to Collserola Natural Park.

The technical phase required a landscape integration study, a mandatory document for projects on protected land, as well as the processing of permits with the relevant municipal authority and the Natural Park Consortium. Construction was executed with the Eskimohaus® system by PAPIK's own works team, and Passivhaus certification was verified post-construction through blower-door testing and energy auditing.

This is the pattern that defines our Development unit: PAPIK assumes the complete risk of the process (plot, financing, works, schedule) and the end buyer acquires the finished product without exposure to construction contingencies.

What K-SANDAL contributes

K-SANDAL is not just another development. For the end buyer, it offers a new home with energy class A and potential for partial energy autonomy, without construction or schedule risk, with ten-year structural warranty and the PAPIK system, plus post-construction support. For the sustainable construction sector, it demonstrates that the Passivhaus standard is compatible with protected natural environments, and constitutes a reproducible case study for future developments on PEIN-classified land. For Collserola, it represents a construction that respects the ordinance, minimises environmental impact, reinforces native vegetation as part of the project, and reduces residual consumption in the protected zone thanks to its energy efficiency.

Frequently asked questions

When are K-SANDAL homes delivered?

The expected delivery date is communicated in the detailed brochure for each home. If the buyer signs off-plan or during construction, the date is closed in the contract. If they sign once the home is built, delivery is immediate.

Is K-SANDAL Passivhaus-certified?

Each home is designed to achieve the Passivhaus standard of the Darmstadt Institute. Formal certification is a buyer's decision, since it adds processing cost but provides third-party validation. The construction allows certification to be obtained when the client requests it.

Can the home you buy at K-SANDAL be customised?

The development has finishes closed in the brochure. There may be margin for personalisation of final finishes (furniture, accessories) if the home is not yet in its final construction phase, but this is resolved case by case during sale negotiations.

What kind of energy autonomy does K-SANDAL have?

Each home incorporates photovoltaic panels and, optionally, a self-consumption battery. Partial autonomy allows essential functions to be maintained (climate control, lighting, refrigerator) during supply outages, complementing the passive efficiency of the structure.
