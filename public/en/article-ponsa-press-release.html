PAPIK Group and the Ponsa family: 2 million euros annually in sustainable housing

PAPIK Group and the Ponsa family structure an agreement for the joint development of Passivhaus projects in Catalonia: EUR 2M of committed annual investment.

The framework of the collaboration

The agreement between PAPIK and the Ponsa family structures a long-term collaboration for the joint development of residential Passivhaus projects. The Ponsa family contributes capital with a sustainable investment mandate; PAPIK contributes technical project, construction execution, integral management and guarantees.

The committed investment of 2 million euros annually is allocated to the progressive development of a portfolio of projects, typically low-volume single-family or multi-family developments, with advanced energy-efficiency standards: Passivhaus certification, energy class A, and integration of renewable energy as a structuring element of the final product.

The agreement is governed by specific legal documentation between the parties, with independent legal advice for each. PAPIK retains its identity as builder and process manager; the Ponsa family acts as strategic financial partner. Neither party presents itself as part of the other, and the integrity of the PAPIK brand is preserved across all projects executed under the framework of the collaboration.

What each party contributes

Ponsa family: capital with a sustainable mandate

Families with consolidated wealth that maintain a long-term view represent an increasingly relevant profile in the real-estate sector. The Ponsa family contributes capital with a sustainability mandate: projects must meet advanced energy-efficiency criteria, quick gains are not sought, and the investment horizon is long with the objective of portfolio building. The agreement was structured following a reciprocal evaluation between the parties, in line with the methodology described in our Wealth service.

PAPIK Group: construction and integral management

PAPIK contributes to the collaboration thirty years of experience building in Catalonia, with more than one hundred projects built and 36 documented on the website with full technical data. The builder contributes the proprietary Eskimohaus® construction system, the integrated team of architecture, engineering, execution and post-construction, and the capacity for integral processing of permits, certifications and post-construction management. This combination allows the Ponsa family to benefit from a single point of contact with the capacity to take the process from plot to keys.

Why this model is relevant for the sector

The PAPIK and Ponsa agreement illustrates a pattern that is consolidating in the sustainable construction sector: the convergence between wealth holders with an ESG mandate and specialised builders.

The factors that explain why this convergence is occurring now are well known in the sector. Regulatory pressure on energy efficiency grows with the European Green Deal and the continuous revision of the CTE (Spanish Technical Building Code); the Passivhaus product has moved from niche to expanding standard; consolidated wealth holders are increasingly incorporating ESG criteria into their investment mandates; and many traditional builders still lack the technical capacity to build to Passivhaus standards, generating a gap between demand for this type of product and supply capable of meeting it.

In this context, bilateral agreements between wealth holders and specialised builders are a logical market response. They provide patient capital to the builder, projects with a clear mandate to the investor, and finished products that the premium residential market absorbs with ease.

Unlike an open-ended real-estate investment fund or a crowdfunding platform, this type of agreement is bilateral between two parties with direct trust, is structured case by case with individual evaluation of each project, operates through a specific ad hoc private vehicle, and does not constitute a public offering or capital raising from the public. It is the model PAPIK describes in its Wealth unit: bilateral service, case by case, in private vehicles.

What it means for PAPIK

The agreement provides PAPIK with stability for the multi-year planning of our Development unit. A portfolio with a clear mandate makes it possible to proactively identify plots with under-used buildability, plan construction schedules with greater predictability, allocate the in-house technical team more efficiently, and maintain construction quality without the short-term cash-flow pressure that often affects one-off developments dependent on case-by-case financing.

At the level of the final product, the agreement guarantees continuity in the rhythm of PAPIK developments. Instead of one-off developments dependent on the moment of the financing cycle, there is a planned portfolio flow that develops progressively over the years.

What it means for the market

For the end buyer of a PAPIK development, the agreement does not change the experience. The buyer continues to acquire a finished product with the same guarantees, the same construction system and the same finishes. The difference, from the buyer's point of view, is that the supply of PAPIK developments on the market remains constant rather than cyclical, and that reduces uncertainty about future availability.

For the sector in general, the agreement is a reproducible case study. It shows that the combination of "patient capital with a sustainable mandate plus a builder with technical capacity" can be structured without depending on speculative funds or public investment vehicles, keeping construction quality control in the hands of the specialised builder and the investment decision in the hands of the financial partner.

Frequently asked questions

Does the agreement change the price or conditions for an end buyer of a PAPIK development?

No. The end buyer signs a deed of sale directly with PAPIK, and the conditions are the standard ones for each development. The financial partner does not appear in the deed of sale and is not visible to the buyer.

Why 2 million euros annually and not more or less?

The figure reflects the joint capacity agreed between the parties for the first cooperation cycle. It allows the development of a consistent portfolio without saturating PAPIK's operational capacity or excessively concentrating risk for the Ponsa family, at a figure that both parties consider optimal for the current moment.

Is the agreement exclusive? Can PAPIK work with other wealth holders?

Yes. PAPIK maintains strategic relationships with various investor profiles in the wealth segment. The agreement with the Ponsa family is one of several private vehicles we structure case by case, within our Wealth unit.

Can the Ponsa family resell its position?

The governance of the vehicle is regulated by the legal documentation of the agreement, with independent legal advice for both parties. No specific governance details are published.
