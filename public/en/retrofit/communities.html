BLOCK 2 · PROGRAMA 3 NGEU · KEY EDUCATIONAL BLOCK

``` What Programa 3 is and why it is the opportunity for your homeowner association

Educational block, public information, legal basis cited. ```

2.1 What Programa 3 NGEU is

`` Programa 3 of the Next Generation EU funds finances the energy retrofit of existing residential buildings. It is the programme with the highest subsidy percentages available for homeowner associations. ``

2.2 What % of subsidy your homeowner association can obtain

`` Subsidy percentages vary according to the energy savings achieved. A retrofit with savings between 30 and 45% qualifies for 40% of the eligible cost. A retrofit with savings between 45 and 60% qualifies for 65%. A retrofit with savings above 60% qualifies for 80%. These ranges are based on the regulatory framework currently in force, and each case is assessed individually based on the specific project and the building's initial consumption. ``

2.3 What requirements the building must meet

`` Eligibility depends on several requirements converging. The building must be an existing collective residential building. It must meet a minimum age according to current regulations (indicatively prior to 2008, with exceptions). It requires an association vote with the corresponding legal majority. It must meet the minimum energy savings targets required by the programme. The Building Technical Inspection (ITE) must be in force or already filed. ``

2.4 Current deadline

`` Programa 3 currently has 31 December 2026 as its horizon, with previous extensions already applied. Our recommendation is to verify the deadline in force for each specific project to guarantee applicability. ``

2.5 Quorum required at the association vote

`` For energy improvements that draw on public subsidies, the Spanish Horizontal Property Act (Ley 49/1960), article 17.2, requires a three-fifths majority quorum of owners and of their participation quotas. In some cases a simple majority applies. Each specific case must be reviewed with the property administrator and the association's legal documentation. ``

BLOCK 3 · TYPES OF INTERVENTION IN HOMEOWNER ASSOCIATIONS

``` Typical interventions in multi-family buildings

Six interventions, combinable depending on the case.

```

`` Typical interventions in multi-family buildings cover six main areas. External thermal insulation of the facade (SATE) is the most common and the highest-impact one: it can reduce energy consumption per dwelling by between 30 and 50% depending on the initial condition. The replacement of common windows is concentrated in stairwells, lobbies and shared terraces. Roof insulation is especially relevant in buildings with a top floor suffering high heat losses. Replacing the communal boiler with an aerothermal system combines savings on the shared utility bill with a transition away from fossil fuel. Photovoltaic installation on the roof powers shared services (lift, lighting, common areas). Full retrofit of the building envelope combines all of the above and makes it possible to reach the maximum 80% subsidy if the minimum required energy savings are achieved. ``

BLOCK 8 · FAQ · 9 QUESTIONS

``` Frequently asked questions

```

``` Q · What majority does the association vote need to approve a retrofit with subsidy?

A · For energy improvements that draw on public subsidies, the Spanish Horizontal Property Act (Ley 49/1960) requires a three-fifths majority quorum of owners and of their participation quotas (article 17.2). In some cases a simple majority applies. Each specific case must be reviewed with the property administrator and the association's legal documentation.

──────────────

Q · How long does the process take from the first meeting to the start of works?

A · Usually between 4 and 8 months. It includes diagnostic, proposal, association vote, subsidy application and contracting. The works themselves can last from 4 to 12 months depending on scope.

──────────────

Q · What if the subsidy is not approved?

A · The project remains viable if the homeowner association decides to proceed with private financing. Many financial institutions offer specific lines for retrofit with preferential terms. We guide you through the available options.

──────────────

Q · Can the retrofit be done with residents living in the building?

A · Yes. A facade retrofit (SATE) or a roof retrofit is done with residents at home. A more invasive intervention (replacement of drainpipes, for example) requires phased planning. We coordinate it with the property administrator to minimise disruption.

──────────────

Q · What happens with residents who do not want to pay the special levy?

A · If the association approves with the corresponding legal majority, the decision is binding. Non-voters and those who vote against also pay. For contentious cases, your property administrator's legal department has the applicable procedures available.

──────────────

Q · Do you work with property administrators on a stable basis?

A · Yes. We have recurring property administrator collaborators. If your property administrator does not yet know us, we can introduce ourselves directly. They will decide if we are a good fit.

──────────────

Q · What about catalogued or heritage-protected buildings?

A · They can be retrofitted as well, but with compliance with heritage regulations. Each case requires specific architectural assessment and often a prior favourable report from the competent authority.

──────────────

Q · Is there a building age limit?

A · The NGEU programme has its own requirements. Generally, residential buildings prior to a reference year are eligible. We verify this during the diagnostic.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between your retrofit and a conventional retrofit?

A · We work with advanced energy efficiency standards (Eskimohaus® system, Passivhaus, EnerPHit). One of our retrofits does not just meet the minimum regulatory requirement, it seeks the maximum possible savings for the residents. ```
