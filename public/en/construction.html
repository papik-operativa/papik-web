BLOCK 3 · ESKIMOHAUS® SYSTEM

``` Eskimohaus®, our proprietary construction system

After thirty years building in Catalonia, we have developed a system that combines the principles of the German Passivhaus standard with Mediterranean materials and climate. It is not about applying an imported technology, but about integrating the international methodology with the specific conditions of the territory.

Four principles, nothing superfluous

```

Column 1 · THERMAL ENVELOPE

`` Continuous insulation, eliminating thermal bridges. We reduce energy losses before thinking about how to generate energy. ``

Column 2 · CONTROLLED AIRTIGHTNESS

`` Verified through blower-door testing. An airtight home is not a home that does not breathe; it is a home that controls how it breathes. ``

Column 3 · VENTILATION WITH HEAT RECOVERY

`` Constant fresh air with no energy loss. Zehnder heat-recovery systems that keep the home healthy. ``

Column 4 · EFFICIENT STRUCTURE

`` Cross-laminated timber (CLT), light timber framing or hybrid systems. Natural, renewable materials with minimal impact on the plot. ``

`` An Eskimohaus® home consumes up to 90% less energy than a conventional construction. ``

What we evaluate on every plot ```

`` The technical evaluation of a plot covers solar orientation and exposure, slope and earthworks required, accessibility for construction machinery, the urban planning framework (the local urban plan or POUM, municipal ordinances, minimum parcel size), available utilities (water, electricity, fibre optic), and geotechnical conditions including possible constraints such as radon, water table or non-consolidated soil. Each of these factors has direct implications on the viability of the project and on the final cost. ``

BLOCK 8 · FAQ · 8 QUESTIONS

``` Frequently asked questions

```

``` Q · How long does it take to build a home with PAPIK?

A · It depends on the project. The design phase can take between three and eight months. Construction itself, between nine and eighteen. Each case has its own schedule, defined before the contract is signed.

──────────────

Q · How much does a Passivhaus home cost per square metre?

A · The price varies depending on the plot, size, finishes and degree of certification. You can configure your home in our simulator to obtain an indicative estimate adapted to your specific case.

──────────────

Q · Do I have to certify the home as Passivhaus?

A · It is not mandatory. Every PAPIK build meets high energy efficiency standards. Formal certification by the Passivhaus Institut is a client decision, since it adds processing cost but provides third-party validation and market revaluation.

──────────────

Q · Does PAPIK build outside Catalonia?

A · We work regularly in Catalonia, the Balearic Islands and Andorra. Other locations are studied case by case if the project justifies it.

──────────────

Q · What if I have a floor plan idea or my own architect?

A · We integrate it into the process. Your idea is the starting point. If you already work with an architect, we can coordinate as the contractor. If not, our in-house architecture department develops the project from the first sketch.

──────────────

Q · What happens after handover?

A · We provide a house-specific operating manual, the mandatory ten-year structural warranty and continuous post-works support during the first year. If adjustments are needed, we resolve them.

──────────────

Q · Can the home be financed with a green or energy-efficient mortgage?

A · Yes. We work with several financial institutions that offer mortgages with bonuses for high energy efficiency homes. We can present the technical project to the bank to facilitate approval.

──────────────

Q · Are there visits to ongoing construction sites?

A · Yes. We organise visits to show-homes and to active construction sites by prior arrangement. It is common for clients to visit other projects of ours before signing anything, and we recommend it as part of the mutual evaluation process. ```
