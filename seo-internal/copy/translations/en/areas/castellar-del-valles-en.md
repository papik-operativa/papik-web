Castellar del Vallès is a town of around twenty-four thousand
inhabitants with a stable residential profile and a sustained

Llorenç del Munt i l'Obac natural park, in the same logic of

Pomer, Aiguafonda and Sant Feliu del Racó allow projects with
a generous brief and good orientations.

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Castellar del Vallès
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The local urban plan (POUM) of Castellar del Vallès was approved
in 2010 and has had subsequent specific amendments. The
consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban
Planning Registry (RPUC) of the Generalitat and through the
municipal planning portal.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
The detached single-family typology predominates in Plana del
Pomer, Aiguafonda and Sant Feliu del Racó, with plots that
often have slope or consolidated vegetation. The party-wall
multi-family typology is concentrated in the urban core of
Castellar centre. Non-developable land sectors with high
protection appear in the areas bordering the Sant Llorenç del
Munt i l'Obac natural park.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential is
typically between 500 and 1,000 m² depending on the sub-zone,
often larger in sectors bordering protected land. Net buildable
capacity ranges between 0.25 and 0.45 m²st/m²s. Maximum
occupancy is between 20% and 30%. Standard height is ground
floor plus one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are
4 to 6 m from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary.
```

### Landscape restrictions

```
Sectors bordering the Sant Llorenç del Munt i l'Obac natural
park have specific landscape restrictions and may require a
favourable report from the park's governing body. Consolidated
trees are protected by municipal ordinance in certain areas,
with an obligation to maintain specimens within the plot when
building takes place there.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Castellar e-government portal
integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC).
Indicative resolution times range between 6 and 14 weeks, with
possible extension if sectoral consultations are involved. The
ICIO tax applies according to the current Tax Ordinance.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

## BLOCK 4 · NOTABLE AREAS OF CASTELLAR

```
[H2]
Residential areas of the municipality

[Editorial text]

CASTELLAR CENTRE concentrates party-wall multi-family and
consolidated single-family housing, with an active retail
fabric. PLANA DEL POMER is a single-family residential sector
with medium and large plots, gentle topography and good
orientation. AIGUAFONDA is low residential with wide plots and
a more rural character, often with consolidated trees. SANT
FELIU DEL RACÓ is the nucleus most tied to the natural park,
with projects that demand careful landscape integration and a
brief consistent with the surroundings.
```

## BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 5 QUESTIONS

```
Q · Do you usually work in Castellar del Vallès?

A · Yes. We have a direct case in the municipality (K-Cim) and
several projects in the county: K-Matadepera, K-Gorgs and
others. We work regularly across Vallès Occidental.

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Castellar?

A · It depends on the area. In detached single-family it is
typically between 500 and 1,000 m². In sectors bordering
protected land, plots tend to be larger. The specific planning
data sheet defines the exact parameter.

──────────────

Q · How does the proximity to the Sant Llorenç del Munt natural park affect a project?

A · Bordering sectors may have volumetric and finishing
restrictions, and in some cases require a favourable report
from the park's governing body. Working with a team that knows
these conditions saves months of permit handling.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Castellar?

A · Resolution usually takes between 6 and 14 weeks from the
complete submission. The deadline can be extended if heritage
or natural environment sectoral consultations are involved.

──────────────

Q · Can Passivhaus be built on sloped plots?

A · Yes. Several of our cases in Vallès Occidental have
significant slope, K-Cim included. Passivhaus and Eskimohaus
systems adapt to varied topographies without compromising
performance.
```
