## BLOCK 1 · WHY TIANA

```
[H2]
Why build in Tiana now

Tiana is a municipality of around nine thousand residents
located just above Badalona, at the southern edge of the
Maresme. Its small scale and proximity to the Serralada de
Marina Natural Park have preserved a low-density single-family
residential profile that is uncommon in municipalities so well
connected with Barcelona. Direct access to the C-31 motorway
and proximity to the Badalona metro place it fifteen minutes
from the city centre.

The residential market is concentrated on plots typically
between 600 and 2,000 m², with detached typologies and a
topography that ranges from gentle to steep. Families who
choose Tiana value the combination of village scale, fast
metropolitan access and direct contact with protected land.
The planning framework is demanding on landscape integration,
which conditions any residential project in the municipality.

PAPIK does not yet have a direct case in Tiana. Would you
like to be the first Passivhaus project in the municipality?
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Tiana
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The current planning framework in Tiana incorporates protection
of the forest land and landscape integration as structural
elements. Proximity to the PEIN of the Serralada de Marina
conditions the parameters of bordering plots. The consolidated
version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry
(RPUC) of the Generalitat (Catalan government) and the
municipal planning portal.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
The detached single-family typology predominates throughout
the municipality, with mid-size and large plots. Density is
low and residential areas are integrated with the natural
hill environment. A significant portion of the municipal
territory is non-developable land under PEIN protection,
which clearly delimits the buildable zones of the municipality.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential
is typically between 600 and 1,200 m² depending on the sub-
zone, with sectors reaching 2,000 m². Net buildable capacity
ranges between 0.2 and 0.4 m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy is
between 20% and 30%. Standard height is ground floor plus
one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are 4 to 5 m
from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary. Plots
bordering the PEIN may have more restrictive parameters.
```

### Landscape restrictions

```
Integration with the Serralada de Marina landscape is the
main planning axis in Tiana. Sectors bordering PEIN-protected
land have strict restrictions on volume, materials, finishes
and treatment of walls. Consolidated native vegetation is
protected by municipal ordinance across virtually the entire
municipal area. Any intervention in these areas requires
specific landscape justification.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Tiana e-government portal
integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC).
Indicative resolution times range between 8 and 16 weeks,
with possible extension if heritage or landscape sectoral
consultations intervene, which are fairly common given the
proximity to the PEIN. The ICIO tax applies according to the
current Tax Ordinance.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

```
Residential areas of the municipality

THE OLD TOWN concentrates the most consolidated fabric, with
between-walls multi-family typology and moderately sized
single-family homes. CAN GAIETÀ and EL DAU are hilltop
residential sectors with wide plots, open views and very low
density. CAN MATAS keeps a calm residential character with
consolidated vegetation and plots that usually have direct
or close contact with protected land. SANT CEBRIÀ is a
transition sector with detached typologies and a good
```

```
Q · Do you work regularly in Tiana?

A · We do not yet have a direct case in Tiana, but we work

K-Argentona, K-Llavaneres. Our site logistics in the county

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Tiana?

A · It depends on the sub-zone. In detached single-family it
is typically between 600 and 1,200 m², with sectors reaching
2,000 m². Plots bordering the PEIN may have more restrictive
parameters. The specific planning data sheet defines the
exact parameter applicable.

──────────────

Q · How does proximity to the PEIN affect my project?

A · It depends on the exact location of the plot. Plots
bordering or close to the PEIN have specific restrictions on
volume, materials and finishes. Landscape justification is
common and the permit timeline may be longer.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Tiana?

A · Resolution usually takes between 8 and 16 weeks from
complete submission of the documentation, with possible
extension if sectoral consultations intervene, which are
fairly common in this municipality.

──────────────

Q · Can you build Passivhaus while respecting landscape restrictions?

A · Yes. The Passivhaus system is independent of the formal
exterior expression. We adapt volumes, materials and finishes
to the requirements of the local urban plan and the landscape
integration the municipality demands, while keeping certifiable
energy performance.
```
