## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS OF MATADEPERA

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Matadepera

[Subhead · 1 line]
Singular restrictions due to proximity to the Natural Park.
Projects in Matadepera require landscape sensitivity.
```

### Applicable POUM

```
The local urban plan (POUM) of Matadepera (approximately 2010,
with amendments) regulates the entire municipal area. The
consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban
Planning Registry (RPUC). A large part of the territory is
influenced by the Special Plan of the Sant Llorenç del Munt and
l'Obac Natural Park (PNSLLM).
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
The detached single-family typology predominates in the
municipality as a premium residential characteristic. The
multi-family typology is limited and concentrated in the central
historic core. Non-developable land is extensive, driven by
proximity to the PNSLLM. Rural land of landscape interest occupies
significant areas of the municipal territory.
```

### Common planning parameters

```
The minimum plot size in single-family residential is typically
between 600 and 1,000 m². Buildable capacity ranges between 0.3
and 0.5 m²st/m²s depending on the area. Maximum occupancy is
between 20% and 30%. Height is usually ground floor plus one with
roof, limited by ordinance. Standard setbacks are 5 m or more
from the road and 5 m from the boundary in many areas.
```

### Specific restrictions · PNSLLM Special Plan

```
Matadepera has specific ordinances due to its landscape context.
The façade colour must be in the earth tone palette by municipal
ordinance. A pitched roof with ceramic tile or similar is
mandatory in many areas, with no flat roofs allowed. Fencing must
adopt typologies adapted to the landscape, with native vegetation
mandatory as a natural enclosure. There are extra setbacks from
adjacent non-developable land. The 25 m perimeter wildfire
protection strip is mandatory on the plot. Projects close to the
park boundary may require a mandatory report from the Natural
Park Board, managed by the Diputació de Barcelona.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Matadepera e-government portal
integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC).
Indicative resolution times range between 4 and 12 weeks, and
may extend if a Board report is required. Additional documentation
may include a landscape integration study for projects close to
the park.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet and consult
the municipal technician or seek direct guidance from the PNSLLM
Board if the plot is in the park's perimeter zone.

## BLOCK 4 · INTEGRATION PHILOSOPHY

```
[H2]
Building with respect for the landscape

[Subhead · 1 line]
Integration, not imposition.

[Editorial text]
```

```
Our construction methodology in Matadepera revolves around five
principles. Solar orientation and use maximises the insolation
of the foothills of the massif for passive efficiency, working
with orientation, shading and natural ventilation. The palette
of materials and colours is kept in a range coherent with the
surroundings (timber, local stone, earth tones) to comply with
the ordinance and to respect the context. Native vegetation
(young pines, holm oak groves, Mediterranean shrubs) acts as a
constructive and enclosure element, not as a decorative addition.
The construction systems chosen, in particular CLT
(cross-laminated timber) on projects where it fits, ensure
structural sustainability aligned with the surroundings. The
landscape impact study, when the plot is close to the park, is
done before closing the technical project to avoid delays at
the permit stage.
```

```
[H2]
Residential areas of the municipality

[Editorial text]

THE URBAN CENTRE concentrates consolidated multi-family and
single-family housing, with a family residential market and
walkable services. The PERIMETER URBANISATIONS (Camí de la Font,
Sud area, Pinetons) offer wide plots with proximity to the park,
forming the premium residential sectors of the municipality. The
PNSLLM SURROUNDINGS concentrate the sectors bordering the park,
subject to extra restrictions, where construction is possible
with a project adapted to the landscape requirements.
```

## BLOCK 6 · LOCAL FAQ · 7 QUESTIONS

```
Q · What minimum plot size does Matadepera require?

A · In single-family residential, typically between 600 and
1,000 m². The specific planning data sheet defines the exact
parameter depending on the area.

──────────────

Q · Are there restrictions on materials and façade colours?

A · Yes. The municipal ordinance restricts colours to the earth
tone palette and requires a pitched roof with ceramic tile or
similar in most residential areas. Landscape integration is
mandatory, not a recommendation.

──────────────

Q · What is the PNSLLM Special Plan and how does it affect me?

A · The Special Plan of the Sant Llorenç del Munt and l'Obac
Natural Park regulates landscape protection across a very
extensive area of Matadepera. If your plot is in the park's
perimeter zone, the project may require a mandatory report from
the Board managed by the Diputació de Barcelona.

──────────────

Q · Do you work in Matadepera often?

A · We have K-Matadepera as a certified Passivhaus case in the
municipality. We work regularly across Vallès Occidental with
projects in Sant Cugat, Bellaterra and other neighbouring
municipalities.

──────────────

Q · Is a wildfire protection strip required?

A · Yes, it is mandatory on most plots: 25 m perimeter with
vegetation management according to wildfire regulations. It is
part of the ordinary maintenance of the plot and is the owner's
responsibility.

──────────────

Q · Can flat roofs be built in Matadepera?

A · Not usually. The municipal ordinance requires pitched roofs
with ceramic tile (or similar) in most areas. There are
occasional exceptions in the central historic core; verify
against the planning data sheet.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building in Matadepera vs Sant Cugat?

A · Matadepera offers wider plots, more immediate natural
surroundings and more restrictive landscape ordinances. The
process requires sensitivity to the landscape context. The
construction cost with PAPIK is similar; the difference is the
value of the land and the singularity of the context.
```
