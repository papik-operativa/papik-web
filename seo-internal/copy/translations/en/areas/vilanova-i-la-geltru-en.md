## BLOCK 1 · WHY VILANOVA I LA GELTRÚ

```
[H2]
Why build in Vilanova i la Geltrú

Vilanova i la Geltrú is the capital of the Garraf comarca (county).
A port-city with its own identity, a consolidated urban fabric
and a residential tissue that combines historic centre, urban
expansion, coastal urbanisations and residential areas on the
foothills of the Garraf massif. Unlike Sitges, Vilanova offers a
broader residential market, with notably more accessible plots
and a more diverse range of typologies.

This diversity is exactly what makes it a relevant municipality
for those who want to build on the Garraf coast without entering
the strict premium regime of Sitges. The residential areas (Mar
i Cel, Solicrup, La Collada, Adarró, La Geltrú) form different
contexts, each with its own logic. Proximity to the sea, the rail
connection with Barcelona and a port-city quality of life make it
attractive to a more transversal residential profile.

PAPIK does not yet have a direct case in the municipality, but
works regularly on the Garraf coastal strip with nearby projects
such as K-Botigues (Castelldefels). Our construction methodology
adapts both to a coastal plot and to a city plot.
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS OF VILANOVA I LA GELTRÚ

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Vilanova i la Geltrú

[Subhead · 1 line]
Mixed urban and residential typologies. Landscape restrictions in
areas bordering the Garraf Park and heritage protection in the
historic centre.
```

### Applicable POUM

```
The local urban plan (POUM) of Vilanova i la Geltrú (approximately
2001, with subsequent amendments) regulates the entire municipal
area. The consolidated version is available through the Catalan
Urban Planning Registry (RPUC). Certain areas of the territory
are conditioned by the Special Plan of the Garraf Natural Park,
managed by the Diputació de Barcelona, and the historic centre
has a heritage protection regime that affects interventions on
listed buildings.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
Vilanova combines typologies that often coexist in the same
municipality. In the historic centre and in the urban expansion
(eixample), the multi-family party-wall typology predominates with
consolidated buildings, in some cases listed. The neighbourhoods
of Mar i Cel, Adarró and La Geltrú have a more modern residential
fabric of multi-family and single-family party-wall housing.
Solicrup and La Collada concentrate detached single-family housing
on medium plots, often with foothill topography on the Garraf
massif. Non-developable land, driven by the Garraf Park, is
present in the interior of the municipal territory.
```

### Common planning parameters

```
Parameters vary notably depending on the area. In detached
single-family residential, the minimum plot size is typically
between 400 and 800 m². Buildable capacity ranges between 0.4
and 0.6 m²st/m²s depending on the sub-area, a higher figure than
Sitges due to the more urban nature of the municipality. Maximum
occupancy is between 25% and 40% in residential. The usual height
is ground floor plus one with roof in detached, and ground floor
plus two or more in expansion areas. Standard setbacks are 3 to
5 m from the road and 3 to 5 m from the boundary in detached,
reduced or zero in party-wall depending on the data sheet.
```

### Specific restrictions · Garraf Park and historic centre

```
Vilanova i la Geltrú has two main sources of additional
restrictions. On one side, inland sectors close to the boundary
of the Garraf Natural Park may require a report from the Park
Board, particularly when the project affects earthworks, native
vegetation or landscape impact visible from the park. On the
other, the historic centre concentrates listed buildings subject
to a heritage protection regime: interventions on façades, roofs
and volumes visible from public space are subject to municipal
technical commission criteria. The 25 m perimeter wildfire
protection strip applies to plots with a forest interface on the
slopes of the Garraf massif.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Vilanova i la Geltrú Town Hall
e-government portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government
platform (AOC). Indicative resolution times range between 5 and
14 weeks, with a wider margin if the project affects the protected
area of the Natural Park or has an impact on listed heritage.
Additional documentation may include a landscape integration
study for plots close to the park, and a heritage report for
interventions in the historic centre.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet and consult
the municipal technician. If the plot is in a heritage protection
area or borders the Garraf Park, also consult the heritage
commission or the Park Board.

## BLOCK 4 · INTEGRATION PHILOSOPHY

```
[H2]
Building by adapting to the fabric of the municipality

[Subhead · 1 line]
Adaptation to context, not a single formula.

[Editorial text]
```

```
Our construction methodology in Vilanova i la Geltrú starts from
the recognition of a reality: the municipality is not uniform.
Building in Mar i Cel is not the same as building in Solicrup,
and an intervention in the historic centre has nothing to do with
a new single-family home in La Collada. This diversity shapes our
approach around four lines. Typological adaptation comes first:
the building responds to the place, whether it is a party-wall
home in the urban expansion, a single-family home in Solicrup or
a refurbishment in the historic centre. Coastal climate management
works with winter solar gain, summer solar protection, natural
ventilation and resistance to ambient humidity and marine salinity,
with adapted construction details. The palette of materials and
colours is kept coherent with the urban fabric or the Mediterranean
landscape depending on the area, avoiding formal gestures out of
context. The Passivhaus standard is applied in full, adapting the
construction solutions to the typology and to the plot, ensuring
the same level of energy efficiency as in more premium areas.
```

## BLOCK 5 · NOTABLE AREAS OF VILANOVA I LA GELTRÚ

```
[H2]
Residential areas of the municipality

[Editorial text]

MAR I CEL is a modern residential area on the coastal strip, with
a combination of multi-family and single-family party-wall housing
of recent development. SOLICRUP is the reference detached
single-family area, on the foothills of the Garraf massif, with
medium plots and topography that often favours views. LA COLLADA
forms another consolidated single-family sector with a family
residential profile. ADARRÓ articulates an urban neighbourhood
with proximity to the sea and mixed typology. LA GELTRÚ concentrates
the founding historic fabric next to the old core, with listed
buildings and its own distinctive identity. The urban centre and
the eixample (urban expansion) host most of the consolidated
multi-family residential. Each sector calls for a specific reading.
```

## BLOCK 6 · LOCAL FAQ · 7 QUESTIONS

```
Q · What minimum plot size does Vilanova i la Geltrú require?

A · In detached single-family residential, typically between 400
and 800 m². Parameters vary by area: the planning data sheet
defines the applicable minimum. Vilanova offers significantly more
accessible plots than neighbouring Sitges.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building in Vilanova and Sitges?

A · Vilanova offers a broader residential market, more accessible
plots and a generally less restrictive planning regime. Sitges is
more premium, with more landscape and heritage restrictions. PAPIK
applies the same Passivhaus standard in both municipalities,
adapting the construction solution to the context.

──────────────

Q · Can one build in Solicrup or La Collada easily?

A · They are consolidated detached residential areas with a
relatively stable planning regime. If the plot is close to the
boundary of the Garraf Natural Park, the project may require an
additional landscape integration study. The data sheet of each
area specifies the applicable parameters.

──────────────

Q · Do you work in Vilanova i la Geltrú often?

A · We do not yet have a direct case in the municipality, but we
work regularly on the Garraf coastal strip (K-Botigues in
Castelldefels) and we have references in the area on landscape
restrictions and the Passivhaus standard. Our methodology aligns
with Vilanova's residential market.

──────────────

Q · Are there specific restrictions in the historic centre?

A · Yes. The historic centre concentrates listed buildings under
a heritage protection regime. Interventions on façades, volumes
and roofs visible from public space are subject to municipal
technical commission criteria. A longer permit process should be
expected.

──────────────

Q · Is a wildfire protection strip required?

A · On plots with a forest interface on the slopes of the Garraf
massif, yes: 25 m perimeter with vegetation management according
to wildfire regulations. It is part of the ordinary maintenance
of the plot and is the owner's responsibility.

──────────────

Q · What kind of project fits Vilanova best?

A · The municipality accommodates well a new single-family home
on a medium plot (Solicrup, La Collada), a party-wall home in
expansion areas, or refurbishment in a consolidated urban area.
The typological diversity of the municipality is its differential
characteristic. The PAPIK methodology adapts to all three situations
with the full Passivhaus standard.
```
