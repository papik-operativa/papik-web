## BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN SANT CUGAT

```
[H2]
Why build in Sant Cugat now

Sant Cugat del Vallès has consolidated itself as one of the
benchmark residential municipalities in the Barcelona metropolitan
area. Its combination of quality of life, access to services and
proximity to the city without giving up the natural surroundings
(Collserola, Sant Llorenç) keeps it as a priority destination
for families who want to build their own home.

The plot market is selective, since residential buildable capacity
is largely consolidated, and prices reflect that maturity.
Retrofitting an existing home or controlled substitution
(demolition followed by new build) is increasingly common as an
alternative to the search for a virgin plot.
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS · UNIQUE CONTENT

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Sant Cugat

[Subhead · 1 line]
Planning framework and technical requirements, public information.
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The local urban plan (POUM) of Sant Cugat was definitively approved
in 2000 and has undergone several specific amendments (Volpelleres,
Can Mates, Torre Negra, Centre Direccional). The consolidated
version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry
(RPUC) and through the municipal planning portal.

The City Council has preparatory work under way for an integral
revision of the POUM, which is why we verify the current status
on a project-by-project basis.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
Residential urban land predominates in the historic core and in
the eixample extensions, with several differentiated zones. The
detached or semi-detached single-family typology predominates in
Mira-sol, Volpelleres, La Floresta (an EMD) and Valldoreix
(also an EMD). Multi-family party-wall and open-block typologies
are concentrated in the centre, Sant Francesc, Coll Favà and the
Eixample. Terraced houses appear in the Volpelleres and Can Mates
sectors. Non-developable land has a significant presence to the
south and south-east, occupied by the Plan of Areas of Natural
Interest (PEIN) Serra de Collserola.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The specific data sheet for each plot is what governs, which is
why these parameters are indicative and must be verified with the
municipal planning service before any project.

The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential ranges
from 400 to 800 m² in Mira-sol or La Floresta, and may reach
1,000 to 1,500 m² in low-density sectors. Net buildable capacity
is typically between 0.4 and 0.6 m²st/m²s in single-family
residential. Maximum occupancy in detached single-family is
between 30% and 40%. Maximum height under cornice is usually
ground floor plus one (around 7.5 m), plus roof. Setbacks are
3 to 5 m from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary
(whichever is more restrictive).
```

### Landscape and environmental restrictions

```
Proximity to the PEIN Collserola implies strict restrictions on
adjacent non-developable land (roofs, fencing, native vegetation,
façade colour). The historic core is subject to a vegetation
protection ordinance for consolidated trees. Urban sectors with
derived planning require mandatory verification before starting
any project, since they may have specific parameters that differ
from the general POUM.
```

### Major building permit process

```
The application requires a technical project, a safety study, a
waste management plan and proof of payment of municipal fees.
The electronic processing platform is the City Council's
e-government portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government
platform (AOC). Indicative resolution times range from 4 to 12
weeks from documentary submission, depending on office workload
and any requests for amendment. The ICIO tax applies according
to the current Tax Ordinance, verifiable in the Barcelona
Province Official Gazette (BOPB) each year.
```

⚠ Every specific data point must be verified against the planning data sheet of the property.

## BLOCK 4 · NOTABLE AREAS OF SANT CUGAT

```
[H2]
Areas where we have worked

[Editorial text · describes each area in context]
```

```
MIRA-SOL is mid to upper residential with plots of 400 to 800 m²,
predominantly detached single-family typology, good accessibility
and consolidated services. VALLDOREIX is a Decentralised Municipal
Entity (EMD) with wide plots (often more than 1,000 m²), moderate
slopes and consolidated vegetation; we have multiple documented
projects in the area, including K-Vall d'Or, extensions and
retrofits. LA FLORESTA is also an EMD, with consolidated woodland,
sloped plots and predominantly single-family housing; we built a
landmark project there combining CLT technology with cellulose.
THE CENTRE and EIXAMPLE concentrate party-wall multi-family
housing, with retrofit projects and new builds on occasional plots;
K-Marges is a recent retrofit-with-extension case. VOLPELLERES
and CAN MATES are sectors with derived planning, terraced and
detached single-family typologies, and buildable capacity regulated
by their specific partial plan.
```

## BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 6 QUESTIONS

```
[H2]
Frequently asked questions · Sant Cugat

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Sant Cugat?

A · It depends on the area. In detached single-family it is
typically between 400 and 800 m², with low-density sectors that
can reach 1,000 to 1,500 m². The planning data sheet of each
property defines the exact parameter.

──────────────

Q · Are there any special planning restrictions?

A · Yes. Proximity to the PEIN Collserola implies restrictions on
roofs, fencing and vegetation on adjacent non-developable land.
There is also protection for consolidated trees in the historic core.

──────────────

Q · How long does a major building permit take in Sant Cugat?

A · Resolution usually takes between 4 and 12 weeks from the
complete submission of the documentation. Times can vary
depending on the workload of the planning office.

──────────────

Q · Where are plots available in Sant Cugat?

A · The market is selective. Mira-sol and Valldoreix have
moderate availability. Volpelleres and Can Mates have sectors
with active derived planning. La Floresta has occasional plots.
We have a network of real estate partners to help you in the
search.

──────────────

Q · Do you work in Sant Cugat often?

A · We have 10 documented projects in the municipality and its
residential areas. It is the most consolidated cluster in our
portfolio.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building in Sant Cugat and a neighbouring municipality?

A · The plot price in Sant Cugat is usually higher than in
neighbouring municipalities (Sant Quirze, Cerdanyola, Rubí). The
construction cost itself is similar; the main difference is the
value of the land and the maturity of the residential market.
```
