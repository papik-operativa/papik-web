## BLOCK 1 · WHY CERDANYOLA DEL VALLÈS

```
[H2]
Why build in Cerdanyola now

Cerdanyola del Vallès is a municipality of around fifty-eight
thousand residents in the south of the Vallès Occidental
county. Its territory includes the EMD of Bellaterra, the UAB
campus, the Parc de l'Alba and a very diverse residential
fabric that ranges from central multi-family to hilltop
detached single-family homes. Direct access to the AP-7,
C-58 motorways and to FGC (Catalan rail) lines places it
twenty minutes from Barcelona city centre.

The residential market includes very different typologies
depending on the area. The urban core concentrates a
consolidated multi-family fabric. Sectors close to Bellaterra
and to the Collserola edge have low-density detached single-
family homes. The Parc de l'Alba areas are in consolidation
with mixed typologies linked to tertiary and research
development.

We have a direct case in Bellaterra (K-Iturbi), which is an
EMD within the territory of Cerdanyola. You speak with a team
that knows the municipality through real on-site work.
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Cerdanyola
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The local urban plan of Cerdanyola del Vallès was approved in
2014 and has had subsequent specific amendments. The
consolidated version is available through the Catalan Urban
Planning Registry (RPUC) of the Generalitat (Catalan
government) and the municipal planning portal. The EMD of
Bellaterra has its own parameters within the municipal
territory.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
Typological diversity is the distinctive feature of Cerdanyola.
The urban core concentrates between-walls multi-family and
row typologies. Areas bordering Bellaterra have low-density
detached single-family homes. The Parc de l'Alba and UAB
sectors mix tertiary use, facilities and occasional residential
typologies. Non-developable land has a significant presence
on the perimeter, with partial protection linked to Collserola
to the south.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential
varies significantly depending on the area of the municipality.
In areas close to Bellaterra and the Collserola edge it is
typically between 500 and 1,000 m². Net buildable capacity
ranges between 0.3 and 0.5 m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy is
between 25% and 35%. Standard height is ground floor plus
one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are 3 to 5 m
from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary.
```

### Landscape restrictions

```
Sectors bordering Collserola have specific landscape
restrictions. Consolidated tree cover in the urban core is
protected by municipal ordinance. The EMD of Bellaterra
zones have a specific regime that must be checked against
the planning data sheet.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Cerdanyola del Vallès
e-government portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government
platform (AOC). Indicative resolution times range between 4
and 12 weeks. Works in the EMD of Bellaterra are processed
under the specific parameters of the EMD. The ICIO tax
applies according to the current Tax Ordinance.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

## BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 5 QUESTIONS

```
Q · What is the difference between Cerdanyola and Bellaterra?

A · Bellaterra is an EMD (Decentralised Municipal Entity)
within the territory of Cerdanyola del Vallès. They share a
city council for general purposes but Bellaterra has its own
specific planning parameters. If you are looking for hilltop
detached single-family living, what you probably want is
Bellaterra; if you are looking for the urban core or sectors
such as Montflorit, that is Cerdanyola outside the EMD.

──────────────

Q · Do you work regularly in Cerdanyola?

A · Yes. We have a direct case in Bellaterra (K-Iturbi), an
EMD within the territory of Cerdanyola. We also work
regularly across the Vallès Occidental with K-Del Carril
(Sant Cugat), K-Gorgs (Barberà) and K-Cim (Castellar). The
site logistics in the county are well established.

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Cerdanyola?

A · It varies significantly depending on the area. In
detached single-family it is typically between 500 and
1,000 m². Bellaterra has its own parameters. The specific
planning data sheet of the plot defines the exact parameter
applicable.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Cerdanyola?

A · Resolution usually takes between 4 and 12 weeks from
complete submission of the documentation, depending on the
workload of the planning office. Works in the EMD of
Bellaterra may have slightly different timelines.

──────────────

Q · Can Passivhaus be built in all areas of the municipality?

A · Yes. The Passivhaus construction system is independent of
typology (detached, semi-detached, row, multi-family). We
adapt the construction system and the envelope to the planning
requirements and parameters of each area, while keeping
certifiable energy performance.
```
