## BLOCK 1 · WHY SANT PERE DE RIBES

```
[H2]
Why building in Sant Pere de Ribes now

Sant Pere de Ribes is a municipality of around thirty thousand
inhabitants with three distinct nuclei: Ribes (the administrative
centre), Les Roquetes (highest density, expansion axis) and a
network of dispersed estates around them. Its proximity to
Sitges, with direct exits to the C-15 and C-32 motorways,
places the municipality in a strategic position: residential
life with its own identity and quick access to Sitges' cultural
and service offer.

The residential market offers a favourable share of single-family
plots in areas such as Vallpineda or Can Pere de la Plana, with
land prices typically lower than those in Sitges or on the
seafront. For families seeking a Passivhaus home in the Garraf
without paying the seafront premium, Sant Pere de Ribes is one
of the most balanced options.

PAPIK does not yet have a direct case in the municipality. You
can be the first.
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Sant Pere de Ribes
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The local urban plan (POUM) of Sant Pere de Ribes was approved
in 2007 and has been subject to subsequent specific amendments,
including sectoral revisions of housing estates. The consolidated
version is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry
(RPUC) of the Generalitat and through the municipal planning
portal.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
The detached single-family typology predominates in the
historic dispersed estates and in sectors such as Vallpineda or
Can Pere de la Plana. The party-wall multi-family typology is
concentrated in the Ribes and Les Roquetes nuclei, with
consolidated urban fabric. Terraced residential sectors appear
in more recent derived plans. Non-developable land remains
protected in the Garraf massif, to the south of the
municipality, with links to the Natural Park.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential is
typically between 400 and 1,000 m² depending on the sub-zone,
with more generous sizes in consolidated estates. Net buildable
capacity ranges between 0.3 and 0.5 m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy
is between 25% and 35%. Standard height is ground floor plus
one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks are 4 to 5 m
from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary.
```

### Landscape restrictions

```
Proximity to the Garraf Natural Park generates specific
constraints in the bordering sectors: volume, materials,
exterior finishes and colour treatment. Some historic estates
have their own ordinances regulating typology, perimeter walls
and vegetation. The rugged hill topography requires a geotechnical
study with stability analysis on many plots.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Sant Pere de Ribes e-government
portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC).
Indicative resolution times range between 8 and 16 weeks, with
variability depending on the workload of the planning office
and any sectoral consultations from the environment or heritage
authorities. The ICIO tax applies according to the current Tax
Ordinance.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

## BLOCK 4 · NOTABLE AREAS OF SANT PERE DE RIBES

```
[H2]
Residential areas of the municipality

[Editorial text]

RIBES CORE is the administrative and commercial centre, with a
consolidated party-wall multi-family fabric and urban
single-family homes with options for retrofit or "demolish and
rebuild" interventions. LES ROQUETES concentrates the highest
residential density and is the most recent expansion axis, with
mixed typologies and medium-sized single-family plots.
VALLPINEDA is a historic estate with wide plots, hill topography
and a quiet residential character, well suited to complete
family briefs. CAN PERE DE LA PLANA is a consolidated
single-family residential sector with good orientation and
favourable topography. The DISPERSED ESTATES around the urban
core offer generous plots with a favourable quality-to-price
ratio, always subject to their own ordinances which should be
verified case by case.
```

## BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 5 QUESTIONS

```
Q · Why Sant Pere de Ribes and not Sitges directly?

A · For two common reasons: the price of land in Sant Pere de
Ribes is typically lower than in Sitges, especially away from
the seafront; and the residential profile is more
family-oriented, with more generous plots. Connection to Sitges
is immediate: 10 minutes by car along the C-32. You get quiet
family life and quick access to the Sitges offer.

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Sant Pere de Ribes?

A · It depends on the area and the estate. In detached
single-family it is typically between 400 and 1,000 m². Some
historic estates have their own parameters. The specific
planning data sheet of the plot defines the exact applicable
value.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Sant Pere de Ribes?

A · Resolution usually takes between 8 and 16 weeks from the
complete submission of the documentation. Sectors bordering the
Garraf Natural Park may require environmental consultations
that extend the deadline.

──────────────

Q · Can Passivhaus be built on sloped plots?

A · Yes. We have experience with rugged plots in the Vallès and
the Garraf. The key is the initial geotechnical study and a
structural project that takes advantage of the slope: usable
basements, stepped terraces, a brief that follows the contour.
The Passivhaus construction system adapts to these constraints
without losing energy performance.

──────────────

Q · Do you usually work in the Garraf?

A · We have a direct case in the Garraf-Baix Llobregat area
(K-Botigues, Castelldefels) and logistics run from the regional
hub. We do not yet have a direct project in Sant Pere de Ribes;
you could be the first PAPIK Passivhaus project in the
municipality.
```
