## BLOCK 1 · WHY PREMIÀ DE DALT

```
[H2]
Why build in Premià de Dalt now

Premià de Dalt is a municipality of around ten thousand
residents on the hilltop slope of the Maresme, just above
Premià de Mar but with a distinctly different residential
character. While Premià de Mar has a dense coastal fabric,
Premià de Dalt keeps a low-density single-family profile,
with wide plots and an urban grid that unfolds along the
slope of the hill.

The residential market is built around detached and semi-
detached single-family homes with plots typically between
400 and 1,200 m². Proximity to the C-32 motorway and a quick
connection with Mataró and Barcelona make the municipality a
consolidated option for families seeking hilltop residential
living without moving far from the metropolitan corridor.

PAPIK does not yet have a direct case in Premià de Dalt.
Proximity to K-Premià and K-Llavaneres allows us to operate
with the same logistical solidity as in municipalities where
we have projects under way.
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Premià de Dalt
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The current planning framework in Premià de Dalt is clearly
distinct from that of Premià de Mar. The consolidated version
is available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry
(RPUC) of the Generalitat (Catalan government) and the
municipal planning portal. The specific planning data sheet
for each plot is the reference document.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
The detached or semi-detached single-family typology
predominates throughout most of the municipality, with mid-
size plots. The between-walls multi-family typology is
concentrated in the old town. Hilltop residential sectors
have low density and good orientation, often with privileged
views toward the sea and the coastal plain.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential
is typically between 400 and 1,000 m² depending on the sub-
zone. Net buildable capacity ranges between 0.3 and 0.5
m²st/m²s. Maximum occupancy is between 25% and 35%. Standard
height is ground floor plus one under cornice, plus roof.
Standard setbacks are 3 to 5 m from the road and 3 m or H/2
from the boundary.
```

### Landscape restrictions

```
Integration with the hill slope is one of the planning axes
in Premià de Dalt. Sectors bordering non-developable land
have specific restrictions on volume and finishes.
Consolidated native vegetation is protected by municipal
ordinance in defined areas, particularly along the perimeter
of the urban core.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Premià de Dalt e-government
portal integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform
(AOC). Indicative resolution times range between 6 and 14
weeks. The ICIO tax applies according to the current Tax
Ordinance.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

## BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 5 QUESTIONS

```
Q · What is the difference between Premià de Dalt and Premià de Mar?

A · They are two different municipalities, each with its own
character. Premià de Mar has a dense coastal fabric and a
consolidated multi-family typology. Premià de Dalt is hilltop
residential, with detached single-family homes predominating
and low density. This landing refers exclusively to Premià
de Dalt.

──────────────

Q · Do you work regularly in Premià de Dalt?

A · We do not yet have a direct case in Premià de Dalt, but
we work regularly across the Maresme: K-Premià right next
door, K-Llavaneres at ten kilometres, K-Argentona. Our site
logistics in the county are well established.

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Premià de Dalt?

A · It depends on the sub-zone. In detached single-family it
is typically between 400 and 1,000 m². The specific planning
data sheet defines the exact parameter applicable.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Premià de Dalt?

A · Resolution usually takes between 6 and 14 weeks from
complete submission of the documentation, depending on the
workload of the planning office.

──────────────

Q · Can you build Passivhaus on sloped plots?

A · Yes. Most plots in Premià de Dalt have a slope. The
Passivhaus system is independent of plot topography. We
adapt the layout, slabs and envelope to the terrain, while
keeping certifiable energy performance.
```
