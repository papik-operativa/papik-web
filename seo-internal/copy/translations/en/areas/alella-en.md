## BLOCK 1 · WHY ALELLA

```
[H2]
Why build in Alella now

Alella is a municipality of around ten thousand residents with a
heritage trajectory that is unusual for the Maresme. The
denomination of origin Alella, one of the oldest in Catalonia,
has preserved an agricultural fabric that gives character to the
residential landscape. Proximity to Barcelona, with direct exits
on the C-32 and the B-20 motorways, places the municipality
twenty minutes from the city centre without losing its
village scale.

The residential market spans detached hillside single-family
homes and consolidated party-wall multi-family in the historic
core. Plots typically range from 600 to 1,500 m², with gentle
topographies and orientations toward the sea or the vineyard
boundaries. Families who choose Alella value the balance between
metropolitan proximity and preserved rural character.

PAPIK does not yet have a direct case in Alella. Would you like
to be the first Passivhaus project in the municipality?
```

## BLOCK 3 · PLANNING PARTICULARS

```
[H2]
What you should know before building in Alella
```

### Current local urban plan (POUM)

```
The current local urban plan in Alella incorporates the
protection of agricultural land linked to the DO Alella as a
structuring element. The consolidated version is available
through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) of the
Generalitat (Catalan government) and the municipal planning
portal. The specific planning data sheet for each plot is the
reference document.
```

### Predominant land typologies

```
The detached single-family typology predominates in the hillside
zones and in low-density residential sectors. The party-wall
multi-family typology is concentrated in the historic core, with
a consolidated fabric that retains notable heritage traces.
Agricultural non-developable land has a significant presence
around the urban core, with protection linked to the DO and to
the vineyard cultural landscape.
```

### Common planning parameters (detached single-family residential)

```
The minimum plot size in detached single-family residential is
typically between 400 and 1,000 m² depending on the sub-zone.
Net buildable capacity ranges between 0.3 and 0.5 m²st/m²s.
Maximum occupancy is between 25% and 35%. Standard height is
ground floor plus one under cornice, plus roof. Standard setbacks
are 3 to 5 m from the road and 3 m or H/2 from the boundary.
```

### Landscape restrictions

```
Integration with the cultural landscape of the vineyard is a
core axis of the plan in Alella. Sectors adjoining agricultural
land protected by the DO have specific restrictions on volume,
materials and external finishes. The conservation of stone
walls, terraced edges and native vegetation is regulated by
municipal ordinance in defined zones.
```

### Permit process

```
The electronic platform is the Alella e-government portal
integrated with the Catalan e-Government platform (AOC).
Indicative resolution times range between 6 and 14 weeks, with
possible extension if heritage or landscape sectoral
consultations intervene. The ICIO tax applies according to the
current Tax Ordinance.
```

⚠ Verify specific data against the planning data sheet of the plot.

```
residential area of the municipality.
```

## BLOCK 5 · LOCAL FAQ · 5 QUESTIONS

```
Q · Do you work regularly in Alella?

A · We do not yet have a direct case in Alella, but we work
regularly across the Maresme: K-Llavaneres at twelve kilometres,
K-Argentona and K-Premià. Our site logistics in the county are
well established and the municipality is fully accessible to our
team.

──────────────

Q · Does the DO Alella affect the construction process?

A · Indirectly. The DO affects agricultural land and shapes the
landscape. The municipal plan incorporates this protection in
the parameters applicable to adjoining plots. On urban
residential land the effect is mainly on landscape, not on
construction.

──────────────

Q · What minimum plot size does the City Council require in Alella?

A · It depends on the sub-zone. In detached single-family it is
typically between 400 and 1,000 m². The specific planning data
sheet defines the exact applicable parameter.

──────────────

Q · How long does a building permit take in Alella?

A · Resolution usually takes between 6 and 14 weeks from complete
submission of the documentation. There may be an extension if
heritage or landscape sectoral consultations intervene, which is
fairly common on plots near protected agricultural land.

──────────────

Q · Can you build Passivhaus while respecting the heritage character?

A · Yes. The Passivhaus construction system is independent of
the formal external expression. We adapt volumes, materials and
finishes to the requirements of the local plan and the municipal
ordinance, while keeping certifiable energy performance.
```
