## INTRO

Building or buying a Passivhaus (passive house standard) home involves an investment higher than that of conventional construction, and traditionally has run into a specific financial limitation. Banks did not differentiate between dwellings with advanced energy standards and the rest of the housing stock, which left the real cost premium associated with high-efficiency standards without financial recognition. That situation is starting to change.

PAPIK Group and Banco Sabadell have formalised an agreement to offer green mortgages to PAPIK clients acquiring high-energy-efficiency homes. This article describes what this type of financing is, how it integrates with Passivhaus projects, and the general framework of the collaboration between the two parties.

---

## H2 · 1. What a green mortgage is

A green mortgage, also known as an energy mortgage or sustainable mortgage, is a financing product aimed at three types of operations: the acquisition of homes with energy class A or B, energy retrofits that improve the rating by at least two levels, and the acquisition of developments with Passivhaus certification or equivalent. The distinguishing feature, compared with a conventional mortgage, is that it incorporates specific bonuses linked to the energy efficiency of the financed asset.

These bonuses vary depending on the institution and on the client's conditions. Typically they translate into the applicable interest rate, into the conditions on fees and bundled products, or into the valuation itself of the real-estate collateral. The intensity of each element depends on the financial institution and on the specific profile of the operation, which is why exact conditions are not published here.

The specific conditions of the PAPIK and Banco Sabadell agreement are private and exclusive to clients who request them through PAPIK. They are communicated individually during the assessment process for each operation.

## H2 · 2. The PAPIK and Banco Sabadell agreement

Banco Sabadell is one of the Spanish financial institutions with a consolidated ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) strategy and an active sustainable financing mandate. PAPIK Group, as a builder specialised in Passivhaus standards, has structured an agreement with Banco Sabadell whereby PAPIK clients can access a preferential green financing channel.

The agreement provides three operational elements. First, a streamlined process: PAPIK presents the project technically to the Bank, sparing the client from having to handle the technical documentation before the institution. Second, formal recognition of the standard: Passivhaus or equivalent dwellings built by PAPIK are automatically validated within the Bank's energy efficiency criteria framework. Third, a dedicated service channel: PAPIK's commercial team coordinates directly with a specific point of contact within Banco Sabadell.

The specific conditions of each mortgage operation, including rate, term, fees and guarantees, are negotiated individually between the client and Banco Sabadell, as with any mortgage product. PAPIK acts as channel facilitator, not as party to the loan contract.

## H2 · 3. Why Passivhaus deserves differentiated financial treatment

A Passivhaus dwelling presents a radically different economic profile from that of a conventional construction, and that justifies financial institutions offering specific conditions. The arguments are technical and risk-based, not commercial.

### Lower operating cost and reduced default risk

A Passivhaus home consumes up to 90% less energy than an equivalent conventional one. The reduced energy bill frees up the client's payment capacity throughout the entire life of the loan, a relevant factor for the financial institution's risk calculation. When two clients with the same income profile take out mortgages on dwellings with different energy performance, the risk associated with the loan is also different.

### Higher revaluation

Real-estate sector studies, such as those published by Sociedad de Tasación or Idealista Data, indicate that energy-class-A dwellings appreciate between 15% and 25% more than equivalent class E or F properties on secondary markets. A real-estate guarantee with better expected revaluation reduces the Bank's exposure throughout the mortgage term, particularly relevant in stable or rising market scenarios.

### Favourable regulatory framework

The European Commission and the European Central Bank actively promote efficient-housing financing as part of the European Green Deal. Financial institutions face an increasing mandate to incorporate ESG criteria into their mortgage portfolios, and Passivhaus housing financing fits this framework precisely. For an institution such as Banco Sabadell, integrating this type of product is not a green-marketing decision: it is an operational response to a regulatory mandate that advances year after year.

### Compatibility with public aid

In energy retrofit projects, the green mortgage is compatible with EU Next Generation recovery funds (NGEU) under programmes 3, 4 and 5. The combination of client equity, public subsidy and green financing generates an optimised financial structure that can significantly reduce the net cost for the end beneficiary.

## H2 · 4. Who this agreement is aimed at

The agreement applies to three PAPIK client profiles, each with a differentiated use case.

### Buyers of PAPIK developments

Individuals acquiring a finished or off-plan dwelling at one of PAPIK's active developments. The technical presentation to the Bank is facilitated by the standardised documentation of each development, which speeds up the assessment phase compared with a traditional mortgage.

### Construction clients

Families building their home with PAPIK and requiring mortgage financing for the acquisition of the plot, the execution of the works, or both. The final Passivhaus certification of the project allows activation of the green mortgage channel at the end of the construction process, once the dwelling meets the standard's criteria.

### Retrofit clients

Owners undertaking energy retrofits of their dwelling with PAPIK and requiring financing additional to the NGEU subsidy. The green mortgage can finance the non-subsidised portion of the cost, completing the financial structure of the retrofit project.

In all three cases, mortgage approval depends on the Bank's risk and solvency criteria, which evaluates each client according to its standard parameters. PAPIK does not guarantee approval; it facilitates access to the preferential channel.

---

## H2 · 5. Process for accessing the preferential channel

The process follows a structured sequence that minimises administrative friction for the client. The first phase is the definition of the project with PAPIK, whether construction, development or retrofit, with budget and expected energy certification defined. PAPIK then presents the project technically to Banco Sabadell with the standardised documentation of the preferential channel, sparing the client from handling this documentation independently.

The Bank evaluates the client profile and the specific applicable conditions according to its risk and solvency criteria. If the assessment is favourable, the institution formulates a specific mortgage proposal to the client, who decides individually whether to accept it or negotiate with other institutions.

Formalisation, should the client decide to proceed, is a direct mortgage contract between the client and Banco Sabadell. PAPIK is not party to this contract. Technical accompaniment throughout the entire process is coordinated between PAPIK's self-development department and the commercial team.

## H2 · 6. Frequently asked questions

### What are the specific conditions of the green mortgage?

The specific conditions (interest rate, term, fees, bundled products) are private and exclusive to each client who requests the option through PAPIK. They are communicated individually during the assessment process, which is why they are not published here.

### Is mortgage approval guaranteed?

No. Approval depends on Banco Sabadell's risk and solvency criteria, which evaluates each client according to its standard parameters. PAPIK facilitates access to the preferential channel but does not influence the granting decision.

### Can I use this option with an energy retrofit?

Yes. Green financing is compatible with energy retrofit projects, typically as a complement to EU Next Generation recovery funds (NGEU). PAPIK structures the optimal financing combination during the project phase.

### Is the agreement exclusive? Can I negotiate with other institutions?

The PAPIK and Sabadell agreement is a preferential channel, but not an exclusive one. The client may negotiate the mortgage with any other financial institution. Our recommendation is always to compare several options before signing.

### What type of energy certification does my project qualify for?

A PAPIK home can qualify for several levels: energy certificate A, Passivhaus certification in its Standard, Plus or Premium modalities, or EnerPHit (Passivhaus retrofit standard) for retrofit. Each level has specific financial implications which we orient in each particular case during the project assessment phase.
