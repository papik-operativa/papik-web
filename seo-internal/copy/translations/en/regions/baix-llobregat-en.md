## BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE BAIX LLOBREGAT

```
[H2]
Why build in the Baix Llobregat now

The Baix Llobregat is one of the densest and most diverse
residential counties in Catalonia, with around 830,000 inhabitants
and a territory stretching from the Garraf to Collserola, taking
in the Llobregat delta and the Montbaig range. The orographic
and urban-planning diversity is pronounced: coastal cores
(Castelldefels, Gavà, Sant Boi on the riverbank), hilltop
municipalities with selective character (Begues, Corbera, Cervelló,
Vallirana) and mid-sized cities with multifamily fabric (Sant
Boi, Viladecans, Cornellà, El Prat).

Connections with Barcelona are exceptional: C-32, A-2 and B-23
motorways, Renfe and FGC commuter rail, plus metro to the first
ring. This combination, with residential land still offering
headroom inland and on higher ground, keeps the Baix Llobregat
as a county of sustained demand for new-build housing and energy
retrofit.

Our work in the territory covers three very different realities:
the hilltop plot with strong landscape component (K-Begues, K-Orio),
the coastal first line with selective aesthetic and salt-spray
demands (K-Botigues, Castelldefels) and dispersed inland development
with a more accessible market.
```

```

Municipalities in the Baix Llobregat where we have built

[Subhead]
We build in the principal municipalities of the county. We have

```

### Begues

```
Begues is the hilltop municipality at the foot of the Garraf
Natural Park, with large single-family plots and a selective
new-build residential market. K-Begues is our documented project
in the municipality and represents our capacity to intervene on
plots with steep gradients, native vegetation and landscape
restrictions tied to the Park's proximity.
```

### Castelldefels

```
Castelldefels combines a coastal first line with selective
character (the beach area), a consolidated urban core and hillside
residential sectors (Bellamar, Lluminetes, Vista Alegre). K-Botigues
is one of our projects in the municipality and exemplifies our
capacity to intervene with coastal aesthetic requirements and
demands for salt-spray protection and Mediterranean-coastal climate
control.
```

### Corbera de Llobregat

```
Corbera de Llobregat is a hilltop municipality with extensive
developments (Can Margarit, Sant Joan de la Reparada, Corbera
Alta) and a consolidated single-family residential market. K-Orio
is our documented project in the municipality and represents our
work on plots with moderate gradient and a landscape component.
```

### Sant Boi de Llobregat

```
Sant Boi is one of the mid-sized cities of the county, with a
dense multifamily fabric in the centre and single-family sectors
at the periphery (Marianao). Multifamily energy retrofit and
selective replacement on older single-family plots have meaningful
headroom here.
```

### Cervelló and Vallirana

```
Cervelló and Vallirana are two inland hilltop municipalities with
extensive developments (Vallirana, Lledoner, Mas Roig) and large
single-family plots. Retrofit of 1970s housing and demolish-and-rebuild
work are common commissions.
```

### Sant Just Desvern and Sant Joan Despí

```
Sant Just and Sant Joan Despí are part of the metropolitan first
ring, with multifamily extension residential sectors and selective
single-family areas (north Sant Just). Typical intervention is
deep retrofit, demolish-and-rebuild and occasional new build on
residual plots.
```

## BLOCK 3 · COMMON URBAN-PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

```
[H2]
Common planning framework in the Baix Llobregat

[Subhead]
The orographic diversity of the Baix Llobregat creates very
different urban-planning realities by sub-area. The POUM is always
municipal and requires case-by-case review.
```

```
Baix Llobregat municipalities have POUMs approved between 2000
and 2017, with frequent partial amendments in industrial sectors
converted to residential use (Llobregat axis) and in metropolitan
residential sectors. The consolidated version is available at
the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) and at each municipality's
planning portal.

Single-family residential floor-area ratio in hilltop municipalities
(Begues, Corbera, Cervelló, Vallirana) typically runs between
0.3 and 0.5 m² of built area per m² of plot, with occupation
between 25% and 35% and cornice heights of ground-plus-one. In
Castelldefels and the coastal municipalities, parameters in the
first line may be more restrictive due to coastal easements. In
the multifamily cities (Sant Boi, Viladecans, Cornellà), the
urban-planning fact sheet is sectoral and depends on the specific
extension.

The Garraf Natural Park conditions the municipal areas of Begues,
Castelldefels and Gavà; the Collserola Natural Park affects Sant
Just, Sant Joan Despí and Esplugues; and the Llobregat river
generates hydraulic easements in riverbank municipalities. The
coastal municipalities (Castelldefels, Gavà, El Prat) are subject
to coastal legislation and to the public maritime-terrestrial
domain protection easement.

Major-works permit applications follow the standard Catalan
procedure. Indicative resolution times run from 6 to 14 weeks
from complete documentation submission, with possible extensions
on plots with Park, coastal or hydraulic constraints.
```

⚠ Any specific figure requires verification on the property's urban-planning fact sheet and the current municipal POUM.

## BLOCK 5 · COUNTY FAQ

```
[H2]
Frequently asked questions · Baix Llobregat

Q · Do you work across the entire county or only in certain municipalities?

A · We work across the entire county. We have documented projects
in Begues, Castelldefels and Corbera de Llobregat, and the technical
and logistical capacity to act in any Baix Llobregat municipality.
Our head office is in Sant Cugat, which lets us travel along the
C-32, A-2 and B-23 with agility.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building on the coast and on a hilltop?

A · The coast (Castelldefels, Gavà) imposes selective aesthetic
requirements, salt-spray protection and summer thermal control;
the hill (Begues, Corbera, Cervelló) imposes gradient management,
landscape adaptation and restrictions tied to natural parks. The
Passivhaus and Eskimohaus® methodology covers both realities,
with case-specific adjustments.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions for natural parks?

A · Yes. Begues, Castelldefels north and Gavà have constraints
from the Garraf Natural Park. Sant Just, Sant Joan Despí and
Esplugues have constraints from the Collserola PEIN. These affect
roofing, fences, façade colour and native vegetation on adjacent
non-developable land.

──────────────

Q · Are there significant price differences between municipalities?

A · Yes. Construction cost is similar across the county, but land
price varies considerably: the first line of Castelldefels and
Begues are the most selective areas. Sant Boi, Viladecans, Cornellà
and El Prat have more accessible markets. Corbera, Cervelló and
Vallirana sit in intermediate positions.

──────────────

Q · How long does the major-works permit take in the Baix Llobregat?

A · Resolution typically takes between 6 and 14 weeks from complete
documentation submission, with possible extensions on plots with
coastal, Natural Park or hydraulic constraints.
```
