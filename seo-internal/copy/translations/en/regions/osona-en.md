## BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN OSONA

```
[H2]
Why build in Osona now

Osona is one of the central Catalonia counties with the strongest
economic and territorial dynamism. Vic, as its capital, articulates
a network of mid-sized municipalities (Manlleu, Torelló, Centelles,
Tona) with a diversified economic fabric: agri-food (a leading
pork sector), inherited textile industry, metalworking and, in
recent years, a growing technology and university component
around the University of Vic.

The C-17 axis keeps the county just over 60 km from Barcelona,
with quick connection both to Vallès Oriental and to Berguedà
and Ripollès. The C-25 (Eix Transversal) connects it with Manresa,
Lleida and Girona. This combination, with affordable land and
recognised quality of life, keeps Osona as a sustained destination
for families seeking primary residence away from metropolitan
pressure.

The continental climate of Osona, with cold humid winters
(prolonged fog on the Plana de Vic), hot summers and significant
daily thermal amplitude, requires demanding thermal envelopes
and particular attention to condensation control. Our Passivhaus
and Eskimohaus® methodology (PAPIK's proprietary low-energy
system) is directly applicable here, with adjustments to insulation
thickness, ventilation management and summer solar protection.
```

### Vic

```
County capital, Vic combines a protected medieval urban core, a
nineteenth-century extension and peripheral residential sectors
(Estadi, Horta Roja, Sentfores). Multifamily energy retrofit is
the frequent commission in 1960s and 1970s blocks of the centre
and extension. New-build residential work has headroom in Sentfores
sectors and on occasional single-family plots.
```

### Hostalets de Balenyà

```
Hostalets de Balenyà is a core in the southern part of the Plana
de Vic, with rural character and large single-family plots.
K-Hostalets is our documented project in the municipality and
represents our capacity to intervene on rural plots with landscape
integration and demanding continental-climate requirements.
```

### Tona

```
Tona is one of the most active residential cores in the south
of the county, with a consolidated detached single-family market
and a proximity component to the Montseny range. Retrofit of
1970s housing and demolish-and-rebuild work have headroom here.
```

### Manlleu

```
Manlleu is the second city of the county by population and has
a dual fabric: compact urban core with multifamily character and
single-family sectors at the periphery (Puig-agut, Verge de la
Gleva). Inherited textile industry along the Ter river generates
occasional reconversion opportunities.
```

### Centelles and El Brull

```
Centelles and El Brull form the southern border of Osona with
Vallès Oriental, with consolidated single-family residential
fabric and the proximity of the Montseny Natural Park as
conditioning element. Typical intervention is new-build residential
work on medium-sized plots and retrofit of 1970s and 1980s housing.
```

### Torelló and Sant Hipòlit de Voltregà

```
Torelló and Sant Hipòlit articulate the northern part of the
county with industrial and agri-food character. Residential fabric
combines compact urban core and peripheral single-family plots.
Energy retrofit and demolish-and-rebuild work have headroom here.
```

## BLOCK 3 · COMMON URBAN-PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

```
[H2]
Common planning framework in Osona

[Subhead]
Osona municipalities share an active land market, a significant
agrarian component on non-developable land and proximity to
protected natural areas (Montseny, Guilleries).
```

```
Most Osona municipalities have POUMs (municipal master plans)
approved between 2002 and 2018, with frequent partial amendments
in agrarian and converted industrial sectors. The consolidated
version is available at the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC)
and at each municipality's planning portal.

Typical single-family residential floor-area ratio in Osona runs
between 0.4 and 0.6 m² of built area per m² of plot, with occupation
between 30% and 40% and cornice heights of ground-plus-one (around
7 m) plus roof. Minimum plots range from 500 to 1,500 m² depending
on municipality and sector. Vic has urban-extension sectors with
specific multifamily parameters and old-town sectors with a
heritage protection regime.

The Montseny Natural Park affects non-developable land in the
municipal areas of Centelles, Tagamanent (bordering Vallès), El
Brull, Seva and Viladrau (in La Selva county). The Guilleries
PEIN and the Cabrerès and Lluçanès ranges define the northern
and north-western frontier of the county. Proximity to the Ter
river generates hydraulic easements in riverbank municipalities
(Manlleu, Torelló, Sant Hipòlit, Roda de Ter).

Major-works permit applications follow the standard Catalan
procedure. Indicative resolution times run from 6 to 14 weeks
from complete documentation submission, with possible extensions
on rural plots subject to environmental assessment or on
non-developable land adjacent to natural parks.
```

⚠ Any specific figure requires verification on the property's urban-planning fact sheet and the current municipal POUM.

## BLOCK 5 · COUNTY FAQ

```
[H2]
Frequently asked questions · Osona

Q · Do you work across the entire county or only in Hostalets?

A · We work across the entire county. Our head office is in Sant
Cugat, which lets us travel to Vic, Manlleu, Tona and the rest
of Osona along the C-17 with agility. We have the technical and
logistical capacity to act in any Osona municipality.

──────────────

Q · What energy standard do you recommend for the Osona climate?

A · The continental climate of Osona, with cold humid winters
and hot summers, requires a balanced thermal envelope, with
generous insulation thickness, particular attention to condensation
control on north façades and summer solar protection. The Passivhaus
standard and our Eskimohaus® implementation are directly applicable
here, with specific adjustments for the climate zone.

──────────────

Q · What price difference is there between Osona and the Vallès?

A · Construction cost is similar. The main difference is land
price: Osona has clearly more affordable land per square metre
than Vallès Occidental or Maresme, making it a viable option for
families seeking large plots within a more contained budget,
particularly on rural land.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions due to natural parks?

A · Yes. Centelles, El Brull, Seva and Tagamanent have constraints
from the Montseny Natural Park. The northern frontier of Osona
has constraints from the Guilleries PEIN and the Vall del Ges.
These constraints affect roofing, fences, façade colour and native
vegetation on adjacent non-developable land.

──────────────

Q · Can one build on rural land?

A · Construction on non-developable land in Osona is heavily
regulated. Building a new single-family home on rustic land is
generally only viable on plots with a pre-existing masia
(traditional Catalan farmhouse), under a reconstruction or
extension regime, and always subject to a catalogued fact sheet
and favourable report from the Generalitat. We verify this
viability case by case before any project.
```
