## BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN VALLÈS OCCIDENTAL

```
[H2]
Why build in Vallès Occidental now

Vallès Occidental (the western part of the historic Vallès region)
is the most consolidated residential area within the Barcelona
metropolitan zone. The combination of strong road and rail
connections (AP-7, B-30, FGC metro and Rodalies commuter rail),
proximity to Barcelona, and a solid industrial and services
fabric (Parc Tecnològic, the Sant Cugat-Cerdanyola corridor,
Sabadell, Terrassa) keeps it as a priority destination for
families building their own home and small developers seeking
land in the territory.

The topography varies significantly between municipalities. The
historic residential cores (Sant Cugat, Sant Quirze, Bellaterra,
Matadepera) have a selective and highly consolidated residential
land market. Sabadell and Terrassa concentrate multi-family
typologies and ensanche districts where energy retrofits are
frequent. Cerdanyola, Rubí, Castellar and Barberà present
opportunities both for controlled demolition with new build and
for retrofitting houses from the 1960s and 1970s.
```

```
Matadepera is the reference residential municipality at the foot
```

### Castellar del Vallès

```
Castellar is a municipality with industrial and residential
heritage, with detached single-family sectors around the core

occasional residential work here and remain open to new
opportunities in the municipality.
```

### Cerdanyola del Vallès

```
Cerdanyola combines consolidated residential cores (Bellaterra,
Montflorit) with a denser urban fabric in the centre. K-Cim and
other documented projects sit in the area, particularly in
demolition-and-rebuild and energy retrofit work.
```

### Sabadell

```
The regional capital, Sabadell concentrates growing demand for
energy retrofits of multi-family blocks and for new-build work
on selective plots within the consolidated districts. We have
documented projects here and a dedicated press release covering

```

### Terrassa

```
Terrassa is the second regional capital and shares with Sabadell
a dual market between multi-family retrofits and selective
single-family substitution. We work occasionally on residential
```

```
Rubí presents a mixed residential fabric with detached
single-family sectors (Can Mir, Sant Muç) and multi-family
```

### Barberà del Vallès

```
Barberà is a compact municipality with a strong multi-family
component and adjacent industrial sectors. Energy retrofits of
```

## BLOCK 3 · COMMON PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

```
[H2]
Common planning framework in Vallès Occidental

[Subhead]
Each municipality has its own POUM (the Catalan Municipal
Planning Master Plan), but there are common denominators worth
understanding before purchasing a plot or starting a project in
the region.
```

```
Most municipalities in Vallès Occidental have POUMs approved
between 2000 and 2015, with frequent specific amendments in
derived planning sectors (Volpelleres in Sant Cugat, Can Mir in
Rubí, the urban axis of Sabadell). The consolidated version is
publicly available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry
(RPUC) and on each municipal urban-planning portal.

The typical residential single-family buildable ratio sits between
0.4 and 0.6 m² of floor area per m² of plot, with maximum coverage
between 30% and 40% and cornice heights of ground plus one floor
(approximately 7.5 m) plus roof. Minimum plot sizes range from
400 to 1,500 m² depending on the municipality and sector.
Setbacks to street and party walls typically fall between 3 and
5 m, with the H/2 rule as a reference for larger plots.

Several municipalities have landscape and environmental
restrictions stemming from proximity to the Sant Llorenç del Munt
natural park (Matadepera, Castellar, northern Sabadell), to the
PEIN Collserola plan of natural interest (Sant Cugat, Cerdanyola)
or to river corridors. These restrictions affect roofs, fencing,
native vegetation and façade colour on adjacent non-developable
land.

Major works permits (llicència d'obra major) require a technical
project, a safety study, a waste management plan and proof of
payment of municipal fees. Indicative resolution times sit between
4 and 12 weeks from a complete documentary submission, depending
on the municipality and the workload of the urban-planning
department. The ICIO tax (Tax on Constructions, Installations and
Works) applies according to each year's municipal Fiscal Bylaw.
```

⚠ Any specific data requires verification on the plot's urban-planning record and on the current municipal POUM.

## BLOCK 5 · REGIONAL FAQ

```
[H2]
Frequently asked questions · Vallès Occidental

Q · Do you work across the entire region or only in certain municipalities?

A · We work across the entire region. We have a consolidated
cluster in Sant Cugat, Sant Quirze, Bellaterra and Matadepera,
and documented work in Cerdanyola, Sabadell, Terrassa, Rubí,
Castellar and Barberà. Our headquarters are in Sant Cugat, which
allows us to dispatch teams across the region with agility.

──────────────

Q · How much does the price differ between municipalities in Vallès Occidental?

A · Construction cost is similar across the region. The main
difference is land price: Sant Cugat, Matadepera and Bellaterra
have the highest €/m² for plots; Sabadell, Terrassa, Rubí and
Barberà have more affordable markets. The urban-planning record
of each plot can significantly affect buildable use and therefore
the final cost.

──────────────

Q · Is the POUM the same for every municipality?

A · No. Each municipality has its own POUM. The reference planning
regulation is always municipal, which is why we verify the
urban-planning record of every plot before starting a project.
The indicative parameters we provide at regional level are common
denominators and do not replace the technical consultation with
the relevant urban-planning department.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions due to proximity to natural parks?

A · Yes. Matadepera, Castellar and the northern sectors of
Sabadell are affected by proximity to the Sant Llorenç del Munt
natural park. Sant Cugat and Cerdanyola have restrictions
associated with the PEIN Collserola plan. These constraints
affect roofs, fencing, native vegetation and façade colour on
adjacent non-developable land.

──────────────

Q · What if I have a plot in a municipality where you do not yet have a dedicated landing?

A · The process is the same. We have proven experience across
the entire region and the local planning expertise that allows
us to work efficiently in any municipality of Vallès Occidental.
We also have a network of real-estate collaborators and municipal
technicians in the territory.
```
