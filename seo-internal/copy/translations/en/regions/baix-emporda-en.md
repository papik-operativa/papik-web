## BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE BAIX EMPORDÀ

```
[H2]
Why build in the Baix Empordà now

The Baix Empordà is the heart of central Costa Brava, with a
residential fabric divided between coastal cores with strong
tourism and premium second-home character (Calonge, Sant Antoni,
Palamós, Palafrugell, Begur, Pals) and an Empordà inland with
historic cores of high heritage quality (La Bisbal, Peratallada,
Cruïlles, Monells). The Mediterranean landscape component, with
the Montgrí massif to the north and the Gavarres range inland,
shapes a strong landscape character throughout.

Connections with Barcelona (AP-7 motorway, the C-32 currently to
Tossa with planned extension) and with Girona (AP-7, N-II) keep
the county as a priority destination for families seeking quality
second residence or premium primary residence. The plot market
is selective in the first line (Sa Riera, Aiguablava, Cala Sant
Francesc) and has more headroom in consolidated developments (Mas
Pinell, inland Calonge, Romanyà).

The Mediterranean coastal climate of the Baix Empordà imposes
specific demands: demanding summer solar control, salt-spray
protection on plots near the sea, tramontana wind management on
north façades and cross natural ventilation. Our Passivhaus and
Eskimohaus® methodology (PAPIK's proprietary low-energy system)
responds directly to these requirements.
```


### Calonge i Sant Antoni

```
Calonge i Sant Antoni form the joint municipality with the largest
residential volume on the southern fringe of the county, comprising
a medieval historic core (Calonge), a coastal section (Sant
Antoni) and extensive development sectors (Treumal, Mas Pere,
Vallvanera). K-Calonge is our documented project in the municipality
and exemplifies our capacity on coastal plots with selective
character.
```

### Palafrugell

```
Palafrugell groups together the inland urban core and the three
most emblematic coastal cores of central Costa Brava (Calella de
Palafrugell, Llafranc and Tamariu). The plot market is highly
selective in the first line and in consolidated developments
(Brisas de Llafranc, Cap Roig). Typical intervention is controlled
demolish-and-rebuild and selective new build.
```

### Begur

```
Begur is one of the most iconic Costa Brava municipalities, with
a protected historic core, coastal developments with international
character (Sa Riera, Aiguablava, Sa Tuna, Aiguafreda) and a plot
market among the most selective in the territory. Intervention
demands particular attention to heritage and landscape.
```

### Pals

```
Pals combines a walled medieval core (Pals Vila), beach and
extensive developments (Mas Pinell, Masos de Pals, La Costeta).
Typological diversity allows new-build single-family work on
large plots and retrofit interventions in heritage-protected
historic-core houses.
```

### La Bisbal d'Empordà

```
La Bisbal is the county capital and the inland service centre.
The compact urban fabric and the heritage component of the historic
core shape a dual market between selective residential retrofit
and new build on peripheral single-family plots.
```

### Torroella de Montgrí and l'Estartit

```
Torroella de Montgrí and its coastal core l'Estartit combine
medieval core, single-family plots in the beach sector and mountain
developments (Mas Pinell, Bell-lloc). Proximity to the Montgrí
Natural Park conditions intervention on adjacent non-developable
land.
```

## BLOCK 3 · COMMON URBAN-PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

```
[H2]
Common planning framework in the Baix Empordà

[Subhead]
The Baix Empordà shares a coastal component, presence of natural
parks and a highly valuable heritage fabric that conditions
intervention across most of the territory.
```

```
Baix Empordà municipalities have POUMs (municipal master plans)
approved between 2002 and 2018, with frequent partial amendments
in coastal sectors and protected historic cores. The consolidated
version is available at the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC)
and at each municipality's planning portal.

Single-family residential floor-area ratio in the Baix Empordà
typically runs between 0.2 and 0.4 m² of built area per m² of
plot on selective coastal plots, and between 0.4 and 0.6 m²/m²
in consolidated inland development sectors. Maximum occupation
ranges from 20% to 35% by sector, with cornice heights of
ground-plus-one (around 7 m) plus roof. Minimum plots in the
first line can exceed 1,500 m² and carry special parameters for
setback, colour and native vegetation.

The Montgrí, Medes Islands and Baix Ter Natural Park affects the
municipal areas of Torroella, l'Escala (border with Alt Empordà)
and Pals. The Gavarres National Interest Natural Site (PNIN)
affects inland Baix Empordà sectors (inland Calonge, Cruïlles,
Romanyà). All coastal municipalities are subject to coastal
legislation and to the public maritime-terrestrial domain protection
easement.

Major-works permit applications follow the standard Catalan
procedure. Indicative resolution times run from 8 to 16 weeks
from complete documentation submission, with extensions common
on plots with coastal, heritage or Natural Park constraints.
```

⚠ Any specific figure requires verification on the property's urban-planning fact sheet and the current municipal POUM.

## BLOCK 5 · COUNTY FAQ

```
[H2]
Frequently asked questions · Baix Empordà

Q · Do you work across the entire county or only in certain municipalities?

A · We work across the entire county and across central and
southern Costa Brava in general. Our head office is in Sant Cugat,
which lets us travel to Calonge, Palafrugell, Begur and Pals
along the AP-7 with agility. We have the technical and logistical
capacity to act in any Baix Empordà municipality.

──────────────

Q · What energy standard do you recommend for the Baix Empordà climate?

A · The Mediterranean coastal climate of the Baix Empordà has a
critical demand for summer cooling, salt-spray protection and
tramontana wind management on north façades. The Passivhaus
standard and our Eskimohaus® implementation, with specifications
for external solar protection, cross ventilation and high-performance
joinery, are directly applicable.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions due to heritage and natural parks?

A · Yes. The historic cores of Pals, Begur, Peratallada and
Calonge are subject to specific heritage protection regimes. The
Montgrí Natural Park and the Gavarres PNIN condition non-developable
land in Torroella, Pals, inland Calonge, Cruïlles and Romanyà.
We verify these constraints case by case as part of initial due
diligence.

──────────────

Q · Do you work with international second-home clients?

A · Yes. The Baix Empordà concentrates a significant share of
international clients, especially in Begur, Aiguablava, Llafranc
and Tamariu. We have communication and contracting processes
adapted to this clientele, with documented site monitoring and
technical interlocution in Spanish, Catalan and English when
necessary.

──────────────

Q · How long does the major-works permit take in the Baix Empordà?

A · Resolution typically takes between 8 and 16 weeks from complete
documentation submission, with extensions common on plots with
coastal, heritage or Natural Park constraints. Municipalities
with high tourism volume (Begur, Palafrugell) may have longer
times in high season.
```
