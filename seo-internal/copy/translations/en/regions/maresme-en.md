## BLOCK 1 · WHY BUILD IN THE MARESME

```
[H2]
Why build in the Maresme now

The Maresme combines proximity to Barcelona with a coastal
residential context and a very characteristic topography between
the Coastal Range (Serralada Litoral) and the sea. The municipalities
of the lower Maresme (Alella, Tiana, Premià, Vilassar) act as a
residential ring around Barcelona; those in the centre (Mataró,
Argentona, Cabrils) combine the role of regional capital with
selective residential markets; and those of the northern Maresme
(Sant Andreu de Llavaneres) offer larger plots with slope and
mature vegetation.

The Mediterranean coastal climate imposes specific requirements.
The main demand for passive housing on the Maresme is not heating
but passive cooling and summer solar control. We have proven
experience in complex orientations, exterior solar shading, and
cross night-time ventilation, all critical elements in this
climate context.

The slope-rich plots common across the Maresme require foundation
and topographic adaptation solutions that have been part of our
project catalogue for years.
```

```
Argentona combines a historic urban core with single-family
```

### Premià de Mar and Premià de Dalt

```
Premià concentrates a high residential density with medium and
```

### Mataró

```
The regional capital, Mataró concentrates growing demand for
multi-family energy retrofits and for occasional residential
new-build in ensanche districts and on single-family plots in
the historic centre and outskirts. We work on residential projects
and heritage retrofits here.
```

### Vilassar de Mar and Vilassar de Dalt

```
Vilassar combines a coastal component (Vilassar de Mar) with a
mountain residential character (Vilassar de Dalt), with predominantly
single-family typologies. We have occasional opportunities for
both new-build and controlled substitution of houses from the
1960s and 1970s.
```

### Cabrils

```
A selective residential municipality of the northern Maresme
ridge, with large plots, slope, and an active substitution
new-build market. Our experience with sloped plots and landscape
restrictions is directly applicable to the municipality.
```

### Alella

```
Alella combines a wine-producing component with a selective
residential fabric. The DO (Denomination of Origin) wine
designation and the landscape component condition planning,
particularly on non-developable land. We work with sensitivity
to the heritage and environmental context.
```

### Tiana

```
Tiana is the lower-Maresme municipality closest to Barcelona,
with a very mature single-family residential market and limited
plot availability. Energy retrofits and controlled substitution
are the most frequent forms of work here.
```

## BLOCK 3 · COMMON PLANNING CHARACTERISTICS

```
[H2]
Common planning framework in the Maresme

[Subhead]
The Maresme presents clear common denominators: sloped topography,
non-developable land with strong landscape value and coastal
restrictions on first-line municipalities.
```

```
Most municipalities in the Maresme have POUMs (Catalan Municipal
Planning Master Plans) approved between 2002 and 2018, with
specific amendments mainly driven by residential pressure and
landscape considerations. The consolidated version is publicly
available through the Catalan Urban Planning Registry (RPUC) and
on the corresponding municipal urban-planning portals.

Typical residential single-family buildable ratios on the Maresme
sit between 0.3 and 0.5 m² of floor area per m² of plot, slightly
lower than in Vallès Occidental, with coverage between 25% and
35% and cornice heights of ground plus one floor (approximately
7 m) plus roof. Minimum plot sizes range from 600 to 1,500 m²
depending on the municipality and sector. Setbacks are typically
3 to 5 m.

All coastal municipalities (Premià, Vilassar de Mar, Mataró,
Llavaneres) are subject to coastal legislation and to the
maritime-terrestrial public domain protection easement, with
specific conditions for plots near the shoreline. Several
municipalities (Cabrils, Alella, Tiana, Llavaneres, Argentona)
have landscape restrictions associated with the Coastal Range
and the corresponding PEIN (plan of natural interest).

Major works permit applications follow the standard procedure
across Catalonia: technical project, safety study, waste management
plan and municipal fees. Indicative resolution times sit between
6 and 14 weeks, slightly higher than the Vallès average,
particularly when there is coastal or landscape impact.
```

⚠ Any specific data requires verification on the plot's urban-planning record and on the current municipal POUM.

## BLOCK 5 · REGIONAL FAQ

```
[H2]
Frequently asked questions · Maresme

Q · Do you work across the entire Maresme?

A · Yes. We have documented projects in Sant Andreu de Llavaneres,
Argentona, Premià, Mataró, Vilassar and other municipalities.
Our headquarters are in Sant Cugat, but we dispatch technical
and production teams to the region with agility.

──────────────

Q · What is the difference between building on the Maresme and in Vallès Occidental?

A · The main difference is climatic. On the Maresme the critical
demand is passive cooling and summer solar control, whereas in
Vallès Occidental the main challenge is winter heating. The
topography also changes, with much more presence of slope and
complex plots on the Maresme.

──────────────

Q · Are there special restrictions due to proximity to the sea?

A · Yes. Coastal legislation establishes a maritime-terrestrial
public domain protection easement that affects plots near the
shoreline. These constraints condition heights, volumes and
materials. Our experience with coastal projects includes managing
these restrictions.

──────────────

Q · What is the minimum plot size required on the Maresme?

A · It depends on the municipality and sector. In detached
single-family residential it usually ranges between 600 and 1,500
m². The plot's urban-planning record defines the exact parameter.

──────────────

Q · How long does a major works permit take on the Maresme?

A · Resolution typically takes between 6 and 14 weeks from a
complete documentary submission, slightly longer than the Vallès
Occidental average. Times can extend on plots with coastal or
landscape impact.
```
