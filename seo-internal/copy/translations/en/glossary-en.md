## Passivhaus core {#passivhaus-core}

### Passivhaus (Passive House)

Building standard developed by the Passivhaus Institut in Darmstadt (1991) that caps space-heating demand at 15 kWh/m²·yr and total primary energy demand at 120 kWh/m²·yr. Certification requires PHPP calculation, blower-door test and documentary review.

See the [Passivhaus construction](/en/construction) service and the [Mediterranean Passivhaus](/en/article-mediterranean-passivhaus) article.

### EnerPHit

Variant of the Passivhaus standard for retrofit of existing buildings, with looser thresholds (25 kWh/m²·yr space-heating demand) that acknowledge the geometric and constructive constraints of the existing stock.

Application context in the [retrofit](/en/retrofit) service.

### nZEB

"Nearly Zero-Energy Building", a European regulatory category defined by Directive 2010/31/EU and transposed in Spain via CTE DB-HE 2019. All new buildings in the EU since 2021 must comply with nZEB. Passivhaus comfortably exceeds it.

### PHPP

"Passive House Planning Package", a spreadsheet developed by the Passivhaus Institut that models the annual energy balance of a building at monthly resolution. It is the mandatory tool for Passivhaus certification and the basis for sizing insulation, thermal bridges and ventilation.

### Blower-door test

Pressurisation and depressurisation test of the building envelope that measures air infiltration. For Passivhaus certification, the result must be ≤ 0.6 ach at 50 Pa (air changes per hour at 50 pascals of pressure differential).

### ach

Air changes per hour. Unit measuring how many times the interior air volume is replaced in one hour. At 50 Pa, it is the headline indicator of envelope airtightness.

### Thermal bridge (pont tèrmic / puente térmico)

Discontinuity in the thermal envelope where thermal resistance drops sharply relative to the surrounding plane (for example, a cantilevered concrete balcony connected to the slab). It causes disproportionate heat loss and surface condensation risk. The Passivhaus methodology requires a "thermal-bridge-free" design with ψ ≤ 0.01 W/m·K.

### HRV / MVHR (VRC in Catalan, VMC in Spanish)

Mechanical ventilation system with heat recovery. It extracts stale air from bathrooms and kitchens, recovers thermal energy at the exchanger and transfers it to the fresh supply air feeding bedrooms and living rooms. Passivhaus-certified units reach 75-90 % efficiency.

## Construction systems {#construction-systems}

### Eskimohaus®

PAPIK Group's proprietary construction system. It combines a timber structure (light-frame or CLT depending on the project), continuous insulation free of thermal bridges, and a tightly controlled airtight envelope. Compatible with the Passivhaus standard and tuned for the Mediterranean climate.

Details on the [Eskimohaus construction](/en/construction) service and the [Passivhaus innovations](/en/article-passivhaus-innovations) article.

### CLT

Cross-Laminated Timber. Structural mass-timber product made of layers of boards glued in perpendicular directions. It enables large-format panels that work as floors, walls and roofs with strong structural and acoustic performance.

### SATE (External Thermal Insulation Composite System)

Spanish/Catalan acronym for the External Thermal Insulation Composite System (ETICS). Energy-retrofit solution where continuous insulation is bonded to the outer face of the façade, finished with a mesh-reinforced render and a final coat. It eliminates integral thermal bridges.

Typical application in the [NGEU subsidies guide](/en/article-ngeu-subsidies-guide-catalonia).

### Modular or industrialised construction

Off-site fabrication of volumetric modules or dimensioned panels that are transported and assembled on site. It compresses the build schedule (typically 6-9 months versus 12-18 for traditional construction), improves quality control and cuts waste.

### Light-frame timber

Timber structure built from small-section studs and headers (typically 38 × 140 mm or 38 × 240 mm) that form a lightweight frame filled with insulation. It combines low weight, material efficiency and design flexibility.

### Mass timber

Umbrella category for large-format solid-timber structural systems: CLT, glue-laminated timber (glulam), LVL and NLT. Distinguished from "light-frame" by its substantial structural volume.

## Energy class and certification {#energy-class}

### Energy Performance Certificate (CEE) A-G

Mandatory label for any dwelling sold or rented in Spain, regulated by Royal Decree 390/2021. It rates the building on a seven-letter scale (A best, G worst) based on non-renewable primary energy consumption and CO₂ emissions. A Passivhaus home typically lands in A or B.

### Passivhaus Institut

Private research institute founded by Dr Wolfgang Feist in Darmstadt (Germany) in 1996. It develops the Passivhaus standard, maintains PHPP, certifies buildings and trains Certified Passive House Designers. Official site: passivehouse.com.

### FSC

Forest Stewardship Council, an international sustainable forestry certification. It guarantees that timber comes from forests managed under environmental, social and economic criteria. PAPIK uses FSC timber whenever the supply allows.

### PEFC

Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification, an alternative forestry certification scheme to FSC, widely adopted in Europe. In Catalonia, a relevant share of locally sourced timber (mountain fir, Pyrenean Scots pine) is certified through PEFC.

### LEED and BREEAM

Whole-building sustainability certifications. LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is North American; BREEAM (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Method) is British. Both rate energy, materials, water, indoor quality and location, without focusing exclusively on energy demand the way Passivhaus does.

## Urbanism (Catalonia) {#urbanism-catalonia}

### POUM

Pla d'Ordenació Urbanística Municipal (Municipal Urban Planning Master Plan), the core planning instrument in Catalonia, regulated by Legislative Decree 1/2010. It defines land classification, planning codes, building parameters (buildability, footprint, height) and permitted uses for every parcel in the municipality.

See the [Cerdanyola-Bellaterra demolition permit](/en/article-cerdanyola-bellaterra-demolition-permit) article.

### ITE

Inspecció Tècnica de l'Edifici (Technical Building Inspection), a mandatory condition survey of residential buildings in Catalonia from 45 years of age, regulated by Decree 67/2015. It produces a report classifying the building as "favourable", "minor deficiencies", "major deficiencies" or "very serious deficiencies".

### EMD

Entitat Municipal Descentralitzada (Decentralised Municipal Entity), a Catalan administrative figure that recognises population centres with limited self-governance within a larger municipality. Bellaterra, inside Cerdanyola del Vallès, is the textbook example in our area of operation.

### Buildability (edificabilitat / edificabilidad)

Planning parameter that sets the maximum constructible area on a plot, generally expressed as built m² per plot m² (m²b/m²p) or in absolute m². It is the decisive factor for the value and feasibility of a development.

### PEIN

Pla d'Espais d'Interès Natural (Natural Interest Spaces Plan), a Catalan environmental protection figure approved by Decree 328/1992. It covers protected areas such as Sant Llorenç del Munt or the Montseny and triggers specific restrictions on construction in adjacent non-developable land.

### AOC

Administració Oberta de Catalunya (Open Administration of Catalonia), a public consortium that provides electronic-processing services between administrations, citizens and businesses. Essential for procedures such as planning permits, habitability certificates and energy-performance certificates.

## Sustainability {#sustainability}

### Carbon footprint

Total greenhouse-gas emissions (in kg or tonnes of CO₂ equivalent) released directly or indirectly by an activity, product or building. For buildings it splits into operational carbon (use phase) and embodied carbon (construction + maintenance + end of life).

In-depth analysis in the [ecological footprint](/en/article-ecological-footprint) article.

### LCA

Life Cycle Assessment. Standardised methodology (ISO 14040/14044, EN 15978 for buildings) that quantifies the environmental impact of a product from raw-material extraction to end of life. Outputs: kg CO₂-eq, kWh primary energy, m³ water and so on.

### EPD

Environmental Product Declaration, a third-party-verified environmental statement under ISO 14025. It documents the environmental impacts of a construction material based on an LCA. Required input for whole-building embodied-carbon calculations.

### Embodied carbon

CO₂-eq emissions associated with the manufacture, transport, installation, maintenance and end-of-life of a building's materials. In Passivhaus construction, where operational use drops to a minimum, embodied carbon becomes the dominant share of the total footprint.

### Operational carbon

CO₂-eq emissions associated with energy use during the occupied life of the building (heating, cooling, hot water, ventilation, appliances). A Passivhaus home reduces it by 60-90 % versus a comparable conventional build.

## Financial and regulatory {#financial-regulatory}

### NGEU

Next Generation EU, a 750 billion euro EU financial instrument approved in 2020 for post-pandemic recovery. In Spain it is executed through the Recovery, Transformation and Resilience Plan, which includes direct subsidies for residential energy retrofit covering up to 80 % of the cost.

Operational detail in the [NGEU subsidies guide](/en/article-ngeu-subsidies-guide-catalonia) article.

### Green mortgage (hipoteca verda / hipoteca verde)

Mortgage product offering improved terms (lower rate, waived fees) to homes with high energy ratings (typically A or B). Indirectly framed by the EU sustainable finance taxonomy. Several Catalan banks market it with spreads 10-30 basis points below the conventional mortgage.

Analysis in the [energy mortgage](/en/article-energy-mortgage) article.

### ICIO

Impuesto sobre Construcciones, Instalaciones y Obras (Tax on Construction, Installations and Works), a municipal tax levied on any work that requires a permit. The rate ranges from 2 to 4 % of the construction-execution budget depending on each municipality's fiscal ordinance.

### BOPB

Butlletí Oficial de la Província de Barcelona (Official Gazette of the Province of Barcelona), the official publication where municipal ordinances, public notices, expropriation files and other administrative acts of the province's municipalities appear. A required reference to verify tax rates and deadlines.

### Habitability certificate (cèdula d'habitabilitat / cédula de habitabilidad)

Administrative document issued by the Generalitat de Catalunya certifying that a dwelling meets minimum habitability requirements (areas, ventilation, services). Required to contract utilities, rent or sell the property. Validity: 25 years for new build, 15 years for second-hand.

## Materials {#materials}

### Cellulose insulation

Insulation made of recycled paper fibres treated with boric salts (fire retardant and fungicide). Applied as dry-blown fill in closed cavities or wet-sprayed. Density 35-65 kg/m³, λ ≈ 0.038 W/m·K. Particularly low embodied carbon.

Full coverage in the [sustainable materials](/en/article-sustainable-materials) article.

### Mineral wool (lana mineral)

Insulation family made from volcanic-rock fibres (rock wool) or molten glass (glass wool). λ ≈ 0.035-0.040 W/m·K, fire class A1 (non-combustible). Conventional product with moderate-to-high environmental impact.

### Cork (suro / corcho)

Bark of Quercus suber (cork oak), abundant in the Catalan coastal range. Natural insulation that is hydrophobic, rot-resistant and high-thermal-mass. λ ≈ 0.040 W/m·K in pressed boards. Net-carbon-negative when sourced from managed forests.

### Hemp (cànem / cáñamo)

Plant fibre (Cannabis sativa) processed into insulation boards, batts or blocks. λ ≈ 0.040 W/m·K. Annual crop with low water demand and high CO₂ uptake during growth.

### Wood-fibre boards

Rigid or semi-rigid insulation manufactured from wood fibres sourced in managed forests. λ ≈ 0.038-0.045 W/m·K. High thermal mass (delays the summer heat wave), vapour-open behaviour and favourable fire performance.
