## Design Context

### Users

PAPIK serves four distinct audiences across three languages (CA · ES · EN) and three territories (Catalonia, Balearic Islands, Andorra). All four are professional adults, 35-65, environmentally conscious, family-oriented, with discretionary income. The browsing context is deliberate, not impulsive: people landing here are mid-decision on a meaningful life event (building a house, retrofitting a home, deploying generational capital).

The four profiles are:

1. **Private clients with land** — landing on `/construccio`. Expect technical depth and long-horizon thinking.
2. **Buyers of finished homes** — landing on `/promocio`. Expect product clarity and absence of risk.
3. **Property owners doing energy retrofit** — landing on `/rehabilitacio`. Expect grant guidance and execution rigour.
4. **Family offices and patrimonial co-investors** — landing on `/patrimonis`. Expect maximum regulatory restraint, sobriety, no aspirational copy.

The site must speak to all four without ever feeling commercially aggressive. The reading session is long (browsing for minutes, not seconds), so the interface must reward attention rather than demand it.

### Brand personality

Three words, chosen by the founder during teach interview:

**LOCAL · TECHNICAL · GENERATIONAL**

What each word commits the design to:

- **Local** — the site lives in Catalonia, the language register is regional, the projects are placed (named after their municipality, never abstracted). Visuals lean into Mediterranean materials, textures, light. NEVER international-corporate-anywhere aesthetic.
- **Technical** — every claim is backed by a number, a certification, or a normative reference. Visuals reflect this: precise type scales, exact spacing, no decorative flourishes that don't serve information. Engineering-minded, not advertising-minded.
- **Generational** — 30 years of history; clients build for their children, not for resale. The interface should age gracefully (no trendy effects that will look dated in 18 months), use materials and forms that suggest permanence, reward slow reading, communicate continuity over novelty.

### Aesthetic direction

**Refined editorial · quiet luxury through restraint.**

References (anchors for the right feel):
- **Hermès** — heritage authority, considered restraint, craft as substance
- **Aesop** — apothecary precision, long-form ingredient lists treated as design, warm neutral palette
- **Margaret Howell** — material focus, generational furniture aesthetic, no logos, no bombast

What this means concretely:
- Asymmetric editorial layouts, not uniform card grids
- Generous whitespace as luxury signal (not as filler)
- Long-form text treated as the primary content (not as a problem to manage)
- Photography of real materials and real homes; no stock images, no rendered overlays
- Subtle hierarchy through typographic contrast, not colour or weight alone
- No marketing animations; any motion is functional or atmospheric, never theatrical

**Anti-references (banned aesthetics):**
- **Greenwashing eco-style** — NO leaf icons, NO green badges or stickers, NO Earth/globe imagery, NO "eco" stamps, NO decorative sustainable shorthand. The sustainability story comes through Passivhaus certification numbers, kgCO₂eq calculations, and real photographs. Visual virtue signalling is forbidden.
- Generic constructora corporate (sliders, big rounded blue buttons, gradient text, drop-shadow cards)
- Real estate agency aesthetics (gallery walls, agent headshots, "luxury living" pull quotes)
- Tech-startup minimalism (Inter, Tailwind defaults, mint-and-grey palettes)
- AI slop fingerprints (cyan-on-dark, purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, sparkle icons, neon accents)

### Theme

**Light theme is the default and dominant mode.**

The existing tokens.css already commits to this: `#002819` (deep forest green) as primary text colour on warm-neutral surfaces. Confirmed in the teach interview.

Patrimonios may explore a subtler differentiation (slightly more sober palette, more white space, no accent colour at all) to signal the regulatory-sensitive register, but it stays inside the light family. No dark mode toggle for v1.

### Accessibility

**WCAG 2.2 AA across the entire site, with AAA where it does not constrain design freedom.**

Concrete commitments:
- Body text contrast ≥ 7:1 (AAA) — easy with the deep-green-on-cream system
- Headings and large text ≥ 4.5:1 (AAA-equivalent at large sizes)
- Interactive surfaces (buttons, links, form fields) ≥ 4.5:1 against their background
- Focus states must be visible and never rely on colour alone
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px on mobile
- Reduced-motion respected via `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` everywhere motion is used
- All language alternates declared via `lang` attribute and hreflang triplet (already in place)
- Form fields with explicit `<label>`, error messages with aria-describedby
- Skip-to-content link in nav

Not committing to AAA contrast on every micro-element (e.g., 3:1 borders on subtle dividers are acceptable per WCAG 2.2). The principle: AAA where it does not require neutering the visual hierarchy.

## Design principles

Derived from the brand personality + reference triad. Use these as decision filters when invoking polish, animate, layout, typeset, etc.

### 1. Restraint as authority

Confidence is shown through what is NOT on the page. A premium constructor does not need to shout. Empty space is structural, not residual. If a design element is not earning its position, remove it.

### 2. Materials over metaphors

The site is about real buildings made of real materials. Visuals should reflect that. Photography of CLT panels, lime mortar, sage-coloured façades — never abstract green gradients. Never icon-as-shortcut where the real thing can be shown.

### 3. Type does the work

Hierarchy comes from the typography first, colour and shape second. The TT Firs Neue family already on the site is the primary voice; pairing with a refined display serif for editorial moments (article titles, project name treatments) is permitted but not mandatory. The serif must NOT be from the reflex-fonts-to-reject list.

### 4. Slow reading rewarded

The interface assumes a reader who scrolls, not one who skims. Long paragraphs are valued. Tables of facts beat infographics. The Patrimonios disclaimer reads like a thoughtful legal memo, not a banner.

### 5. Mediterranean light, not Nordic chill

Whites should be warm (cream, stone, oat) — never bluish. Shadows should suggest sun, not fluorescence. The palette already does this (`#56685E` warm green grey, `#9FA9A3` sage neutral). Push further toward Aesop's olive-and-paper register; pull back from any digital cold-blue tendency.

### 6. Sustainability without symbols

Eco-credentials come through verifiable facts (Passivhaus Institut number, EnerPHit certification, kgCO₂eq, FSC, PEFC), not through imagery. The word "sustainable" is rationed. No "eco" badges. No green ticks. The reader either understands what Passivhaus certification means or they want to learn — both are served by content, not by stickers.

### 7. Generational, not trendy

Every visual decision should pass this test: "Will this look right in 2034?" Trends in motion design, layout fashion, and colour palette change quickly. Heritage typography, considered grids, real photography, and warm neutrals do not.

### 8. Modular consistency, asymmetric expression

The design system (tokens.css) provides the modular grammar (spacing, type scale, colour). Layouts express creatively WITHIN that grammar — never against it. Same vocabulary, varied poetry.

## Decisions deferred to later modes

These will be resolved during `/impeccable craft`, `/polish`, `/typeset`, or `/animate` phases — recorded here so we don't lose track:

- **Display serif decision** — whether to add a refined serif (e.g. Reckless Neue, Migra, Editorial New) for article titles and project hero treatments, or to stay sans-only with extreme weight + size contrast. Answer depends on photography: with strong photography, sans alone may suffice; without, a serif provides the editorial gravitas.
- **Patrimonis register differentiation** — how subtly the B2B page signals "different audience" without breaking site cohesion (probably: extra white space, no sage accent, sterner type weights).
- **Project page hero treatment** — full-bleed photo vs typographic-first vs hybrid. To be decided once curated photography arrives.
- **Asymmetric composition rules** — when to break the grid for emphasis (homepage hero, article openings, key pull quotes). To be designed during craft mode.
- **Motion choreography** — single high-impact entrance per page (homepage hero stagger, article reveal) vs scattered micro-interactions. Default toward the former.
